She had made severall times in her sicknesse uppon occa
-sion of ministers vissitting her: many very excelent
confessions of her faith, & proffession of those Christian
foundations uppon which our faith was built, & of her
true Zeale to the service of God in his wholy ordenances
of our most Pieous & Christian Church of England
wishing us, & chargeing, as we would Escape the danger
of Damnation. not to dishonour that great God whom we
served by reouncincing that faith & Profession which
was taught us by the holy Cleargy & Bushopps of
England. never to listen to the insinuations of any
factious new doctrines what ever. but serve God
truly and sincerely therein. & he would accept of our
Soules, & we should be happie; if not in this world; yet
hoped in a better: And that she did beleeive, that if we
