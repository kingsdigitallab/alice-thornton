In giveing me the praises & teares, comforts & assistances of

my deare husband & Aunt , with my good brother Denton & all

other of my faithfull freinds. who could not be but convincd

of those horible wickednesse in these passages related. &

all endevoured my restoration againe: that my Life might

be spaired to give Glory to the God of my salvation.

One passage more must not be forgotten which my brother Dent

And Aunt was wittnesse of that Mrs Danby beleiving as she

said me, & Mr Comber to be wronged: did solmnely sweare

to them that she would never open her mouth against us

of any thing while she breathed: & since she went away

has endevoured to be reconsiled to me againe that she might

injoy the same privileges as before.

But surely neither Gods Law, nor mans. can obleige me

to entertaine those parties; which has soe highly sinned a-

gainst both. for those wounds are incurable which she gave

& will not leave me to my Grave. Lett noe good Christian

taxe my Charity. the Sins were of such a nature against my

hon.or & Families; that it would confirme those lies which has bin

Against me. And a great dishonour of God & christian Relig

-ion: & therefore not of that private conserne, with in the verge of

my private Charity. as of a petty perticuler injury.

Nor would I for the world, Patronise any thing of this nature

who has soe cleare a consience towards God & man.

nor open the mouths of the wicked justly; against the Person of

those of whom I know noe evill.

In this I will not faile that my Praiers may be heard for her

true & unfeigned Repentance: that God may pardon those

great & high indignities against, him selfe, the truth & us

together with all others contributaries, in this Tragiedie

Least they perish in there iniquities.

P Uppon my lamentable abuses of fallse slanders raised

against me. July 20th 1668.

O: Lord God, who hearest the Prayers of them that call upon thee

in theire calamities and distresses, have mercy upon me

thy weake & afflicted hanmaid & servant.
