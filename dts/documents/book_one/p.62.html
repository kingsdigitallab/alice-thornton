My Mothers Preservation from the Irish Rebellion

October Octb . 23 1641. A Thanksgiving Thankesgiveing for

our great deliveration:

Many, & great was the sorrows sorrowes & sufferings of my

dear deare & hon.ord honoured Mother , with her whole Family Familie upon uppon

the sad change, by the death of my Honred honoured Father -

And she continued contineued in her house in Dublin . Mainta

-ining the great household in the same condition condittion as it

was. At her own owne charges, for the honour honor of the same, to

her own owne disadvantage many ways waies .

for she by that means meanes tarried in Ireland , dischar

-ging those Servants & paying paieng many debts, which

should have been bin done don by the Executor longer than then she

could well do doe ; in regard that her Jointure Joynture being beeing in

England she wanted supplies suplies . thus she continued contineued

till about the october after affter , when on the 23d day in the

year yeare 1641 that horrid Rebellion & massacre of the

poor poore English Protestants began to break breake out in the

country countery . which was by the all seeing Providence of God

prevented in the Citie of Dublin where we were weare .
