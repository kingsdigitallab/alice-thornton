Dec. 10 1640. My brother Christopher Wand.

being beeing in the Church called Christs Church in

Dublin hearing heearing the great & dreadful dreadfull cry that

the Irish made at att my dear deare fathers Funeral Funerall

was so soe frighted that he fell into the most grievous greivos

fits fitts of the Spleen Splen which much tormented him for many

years yeares after affter & had like to have taken his life away

butt blessed be the gracious God by my dear deare mother mothr

excellent excelent cares cairs . cost, & pains paines he was cured & became becam

a very strong man. & lived livd to be the Father Fathr of that Fam family

of which he was descended & was my beloved brother

living liveing to the Age of 61 years yeares & died at London

feb. 23d 1686. buried at Kirklington by his ancestors Antien

& hon.red honoured Fathers Last will & Testament in

Ireland in the Rebellion. & by what Providence Providenc

Date Octo October 2

1640 it was found out & our destruction

thereby Prevented:

This my dear deare fathers will was dated october octber 2d 1640

Proved, & left on the file by Coz. william wandesford

an Executor. remained remaned on the file for several severall years

Till the year yeare 1647. when my brother George wandesf

went into Ireland to get a true copy of it & not having

money mony to discharge it The sad will was laid by in a Chest

by the Clarke who had writ it out & thes thus was sececd secured for several severall

year yeare of the sarre after afftr my brother George died & none non knew

what became of it.

Upon Uppon which great fact facte & troubles arose for the want of it

by Sir John Lowths bad counsel counsell to my brothr Ch. wand. who

married marrid his daughter & would have come into the Estate by the

Entail Intaile .

But all at last by the Providence of the great God of heaven heven this

said will was discovered discovd & produced producd in the year yeare 1653 which did

put an end of all the Troubles: This is fully related in my second 2d Book Booke
