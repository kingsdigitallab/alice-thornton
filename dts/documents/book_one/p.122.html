betwixt them.[1](Note) Due to delays in the construction of East Newton Hall, the Thorntons stayed with her mother at Hipswell Hall until her death in 1659, after which they were evicted by her brother, Christopher, who automatically inherited the property. Thornton here implies that an agreement had been made between Christopher and his mother that he would house and feed the Thorntons for three years until their new house was ready. The Thorntons first moved to her aunt Anne Norton's house St. Nicholas, in Richmond, in March 1660, where their son William was born the next month. At some point in 1660 or 1661 they moved to stay with relatives at Oswaldkirk, possibly William's sister Margaret and her husband John Denton, until they moved to East Newton in May or June 1662.
