Upon Monday Munday he was not well, and had Pills given him

as he used to have by my bro. Portington but they did

not work worke kindly & so soe had a clyster glister given givin him being beeing the

Method ordered by Dr wittie .

On monday munday he sent for Mr Sinkler . and told tould him. That

he knew formerly he had been bin in much trouble & sadness sadnesse

for his Sins Sinns & walked uncomfortably for the want of the

sense sence of Gods favour, in great fears feares & doubtings.

But now the Lord was pleased to make himself himselfe known knowne

to him to be a reconciled reconsiled Father in Christ Jesus. And that

he was at peace with him; he perceiving perceaving a great deal deale of

Joy & comfort inwardly in his soul soule & mind minde . Blessing

the Lord for this his infinite infinitt mercies mercys . & hoped that he should

bless blesse his name for ever more that he was had been bin so soe troubled

for now he was reconciled reconsiled to him again againe . there, with many

such like expressions. to the great satisfaction sattisfaction of Mr Sinkler

who stayed staied a good while with him.

Then towards night this sweet sweete Saint of God grew worse

& more heavy & drowsy drousie according to that distemper. and

they sent to me for the Dr which came to him on wednesday after

dinner. my dear deare was then very weak weake in body bodie . but I

bless blesse God perfect in mind & understanding. Mr Comber

going goeing to see him. he took tooke his leave of him and bid him to

Remember him to his dear deare wife. bid me be patient &

contented with Gods hand, & to submit submitt to his will. which he uttered uttrd

as well as he could for his speech speach being beeing taken. After which he

had his hair haire taken of by order, being beeing the last remedy remidie . & this

with his own owne consent. but Alas Alass no noe remedies remidies , or medicine meadicine

nor Art could prevail prevaile . it being the determination of our God

to take him to himself himselfe . And yet to mix mixe this bitter Cup of

Death with the allay alay of a quiet quiett frame & temper, free from

any torment or signs signes of pains paines lying lieing as if he were in a sweet

sleep sleepe . And by degrees growing colder at his feet feete . & so soe

dieing upwards, & drawing his breath shorter all the Thursday

morning. And towards Eleven o'clock a clocke in the fore noon noone he

Fetched Fetcht one little litle Sigh and so soe sweetly sweetely resigned up his spirit spiritt

in to the hands of his dear deare Saviour & Redeemer Redemer Jesus Christ.
