The Death of Sir Edward Osborne . 1646.

My uncle. Sir Edward Osborne who was my mothers

owne half brother; was a very good, wise & prudent, man

under whose whoes tuition my father left the hope of his house

my brother George , as being joint joynt guardian gardian with my Mother

he had so soe fraternal fraternall a love for & parental parentall care caire over my

Dear Deare mother, & us all; that we were weare most happy happie in

him. & during his life, this our Family Familie was kept in much

peace & tranquillity tranquilitie , he seeing that each party had its

right & dues. with a care caire for the due observance of my

fathers will, of which he was an Executor

Butt after affter his death, we (that is to say) my Mother , & her

Children was much oppressed opprest , & injured, through the bad

Menagerie Managerie of all that Estate, & that was all seized seised on by my

Uncle Wand. for the Debts, which he was much wronged of too,

by one he made a Lease Leace of it for seven 7 years yeares , taking takeing many

100d pounds more than then his due, & before he got gott it again againe

he put him to a suite. but in this time all the Children was

Maintained, by my dear deare Mother , from her Jointure Joynture .
