My deliverance delivrance from Drowning in the River at midlam

when I went to be a witness wittness to my sister Danbys first

Francis Born Borne at att Midlam Castle in the year yeare 1644

At that time Sir Thomas Danby was forced with my Sister

& Children to be in safety safty from the Parliament forces

he being beeing for King Charles the first . to Midlam Castle

A garrison garison under my Lord Lofftus . There she was delivered delivrd

of her first Son Francis Danby . my sister having

got gott my Lord Lofftus , & my Lady H self selfe with annother Co.Branlen

for witness wittnesse . I was forced to go goe over the River near neare

midlam called calld swaile Ure which had some stoops set sett up for

guides & if one had raised the causey Caucy they had been bn irrecoverably erre-

-covrably lost. at that time I was very hearty & strong

& did venture to ride the same or else ellse might have gone gon

back backe & rather rathr than then she should be disappointed disapoynted did venture

over after affter my mo. servant who led the way but it

happened happned the River Rivr proved deeper than then we expected it

& I kept up my horse horrse as well as I could. from standly standing

& so soe bore up a long time. But when we were gone gon so soe

far farre that I could not turn turne back backe The River proved provd past

Riding & the bottom could not be come to by the poor poore

mare maire which was an excellent excelent mare maire of my poore brother

G. wandesfords So Soe I saw my self selfe in such apparent aparnt danger

And begged beged of God to asset me & the poor poore Beast I rid on & to

be merciful mercifull to me & deliver delivr me mee out of that death for J. Cht Jesus Christ

his sake. And the poor poore mare maire drew up her fore feet feete & I

perceived percved she did swim swime . I gave her the Reins Reines & took tooke of

the short Reins Reines of the breech & gave her the head with all the

help helpe I could & clasped my hands about her maine did

freely commit comit myself to my God. to do what he pleased pleeced with me

And she did by mercy bear beare up her head & swimmed swimd out

aboue half halfe a quarter of a mile across crose that dreadful dreadfull River Rier & by

gods great mercies mercys brought me over ovr that River Rier in Safety. which deliver delever

was so soe great & dread full that I can never nevr forget forgett to praise prace the god
