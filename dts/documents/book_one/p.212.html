Upon Uppon my dear deare Joyce her death Jan. 27 1665.

It was the pleasure of our God to visit vissitt my dear deare Child Childe

Joyce Thornton with a great sickness sickenesse falling exceeding exceding ill on

sunday the 20th of Jan. 1665 beginning begining as we thought with a

Cold which struck strucke in many Red spots spotts all over her body bodie & face

after affter which she mourned & cried exceedingly being beeing tormented

with her sickness sickenesse , we used all means meanes that could be done don to so soe

young a child childe by the advice of Dr Wittie wittie . yet yett no noe thing did

prevail prevaile it being beeing the pleasure of God to take her out of this

miserable world.

She continued contineued with some intermissions intermittions of slumbers, & in

much strength did resist the sickness sicknesse . till the thursday af

ter. when it was past hopes of recovery. and about one

& two 2 o'clock a clocke in the afternoon affternoone on Friday the 27th Jan. 65.

The Lord was pleased to free her from all pains paines , by taking takeing

her to his mercy. when she sweetly sweetely fell asleep a sleepe , without with out

any pain paine or extremity extreamity to the Appearance of all.

She was buried at Stonegrave by Mr Comber the next day
