And in the company of Dr wittie . the 8th of June following he went

by a hired coach to scarbrough . And about a month after affter

he returned home on horse horrse back backe . it being beeing his own owne desire:

All which time we had a dear deare & comfortable correspondency corrospondancy

between betweene us, by our mutual mutuall And frequent letters. I hearing heareing

of him, or from him three 3 times in a week weeke & ofter oftener each one bring

-ing me the good news newes of his having haveing cast off his melancholy malancoly

It cannot be imagined immagined , what my Joy was in his return returne

to find finde him so soe prettily recruited recruted by a cheerful chearefull temper

of spirit spiritt . but I quickly saw: that his weakness weaknesse had left

much dregs dreggs of his distemper. yet yett had I great cause

of thankfulness thankefullnesse to the Lord which gave me hopes of him.

& that if he could have been bin prevented of cold he would remain remaine

well.

Servant to the young Countesse of sussex ; and accounts

hard usage usuage by Madam Danby ; & several severall discontents

framed by her servant in my house , adding to her for-

said for my sake. these, with other secret secrett unjust reasons

in their there designs designes & Mrs Danbys undermining underming me in secret secrett

brought Mrs Batte late at night to my house at newton

under pretence of providing butter for Mrs Danby at the

service at att hooly to wait waite on that mad Countesse of Sussex

Mrs Batt told tould me. she came to fetch fech her cosen danby a
