Even at that time did those which had a secrett hatred
against me (though I neither knew it, nor its cause then
for I never in my whole life by word or act had the least pre-
-judice or don her any injury, to make it: as I must

nesse & sad condittion, to some in secrett saing that I ailed
nothing, & I was as well as she. & made my selfe a
talke to my neighbours). All which she carried with much
subtilty for the dishonour of my poore despised Person
sufficiently afflicted with out this addittion. yet were
these but the begining of sorrowes to me upon that account; endeavouring
to bare all with abundance of Patience. which my God did
please to give me: in part hopeing with all that when I mett with
Tom. Danbys wife. I should prevaile for her restoration
to her children; which she did object daily to me that she
had bin kept from, because her sister was angery she
came, as before, to be with me, while I layd lame. But
I still tould her if I had suspected her sisters displea-
sure for that, I would never have, putt that to hazard for the
world. nor did she in the least give me notice thereof.
but my nephewe Kitts wife did make this an objection. & I beelive
owed me noe good will for it. though she reserved more
for an affter game (secrett) to my Ruine.
