Asking Askeing him several severall questions. & hearing his witty

answers, seeing him an Irish orphan , had compassion on

him. and told tould him if he would be willing to forsake

all his old ways waies that he was bred up in his Papist friends freinds .

he would bring him up in the true fear feare of God. And

he would take care caire of him & provide for him that he

should never want all his days daies .

At which the boy was very glad, & said he thanked his Lordship

& that he would be willing to learn learne what he should put

to him. & would pray for him all his days daies . so from that

he took tooke him home, clothing & nourishing him till he hee died

& then my mother continued contineued the same in her house where

he received receaved such instruction & as that I hope the Lord had

glory thereby, & that poor poore soul soule now reaps reapes the benefit beneifitt of

of such charity.
