After Affter which agreements Articles & writings writtings done, there

followed a pretty space, ere his Mother had passed a

fine with him, in regard that his Father in Law , would not

join joyne in the fine. & my Mothers counsel councell did not approve

of it to be legal legall for security without with out it. but Mr Thornton Thorton

did faithfully engaged ingaged to do doe the same legally leagally &

pass passe the fine after affter his death.

but in the interim intrime I was left in an uncertainty incertainty for the

security of a Jointure Joynture in case of his death before me, &

when my Portion was disposed of by him, could have

no noe benefit benifitt thereby. so soe that my Mother could proceed no noe

further, lest least any ill consequent might follow, but

wholly wholely this business bussinesse was Left to my own owne choice choyce , what

I would do doe in this case. she being beeing loath loth as upon her

own owne account, to under go goe such a concern conserne in which

there was such a hazard. wherein she was not to be blamed,

butt Mr Thornton was very much troubled upon uppon this un-

just denial deniall of his Father in Law . by which he was likely

to have undone undon his desires. fell into much sadness sadnesse &

discontent, which perhaps might hasten his Sickness Sicknesse that

he fell into, on his going goeing from hipwell .

his Ague began in the way, to his mothers when finding

himself himselfe not able to go goe through. came back backe to Hipwell

falling exceeding ill into a fever feaver , But upon uppon the advice

of Dr wittie . he was let lett blood, & had all means meanes used for

his recovery. but was brought dangerously ill. at the last

it pleased God that he recovered beyond all expectation.

And upon uppon his recovery, I was willing to rely relie upon his

promise to my Mother , & his infinite infinitt expressions to my self selfe I

rested upon Providence in the fulfilling fullfilling of his desire. in

so soe much that about the December following proceeded our marriage

this is the true relation of this grand concern conserne of my life, which

I have been bin the more tedious teadious in, because I would leave to

Posterity the Right understanding of that concern conserne .
