Wee had undoubtedly perished, beeing drove by
the force of Tempest backe in to the crosse Seas. &
10 houers were at Anchor, beaten on the Sandes
before that fisher man could come neare to helpe us.
at the last, about 8 a clocke at night, we got Safe
To harbour through the infinitt mercys of our great, & Powerfull
Lord God, we landed safely at the shore of the Skirries.
where the same Hubert with all his familie, & freinds
mett us with great Joy, entertaining my mother &
All hers, with abundante affection, & kindenesse.
Which he did uppon the account of obligation, to shew
his gratitude for an Emminent peice of Justice
don him from my father. whoe had decided a
grand controversie in Law suites, which was depending
20 yeares, almost to his utter Ruine. his adversary
being so potent. that he could not gett his cause heard
till that time; when through the uprightnesse of the
Judge, & honnesty of the man his cause rightly de-
termined. & he preserved.

This providence was the more remarkable. that God
soe ordered our ship to this shore, where my Mother
found all manner of releife for us all which we wanted
And on the next day, Came My father from Dublin
in the company of many noble freinds in Coatches.
