March 31
1651 Which when John Plumer, & his fellow, saw the Plott against
them, was in great Rage, against the Committee, & wished they had
never come to them, & repented, them of theire folley, & one thing
was very remarkable; These poore men was the first that ere
found the body of my deare Brother in the River Swale when he
was drowned, in goeing to Richmond, to write to uncle Darley
to returne him thankes, for clearing his Sequestration;

But the occasion of his beeing on the Moore that day was as
followes, my deare B. G. being sent in to France by his Gaurdians
for Education (as most of the English Gentry was) for better Accomplishm
under the caire & conduct of a most Pieous, Learned; Scollers. Tuition
affter my Fathers death.) whose name was Mr George Anderson.
