Upon the beheading of King Charels the Martyr Jan 30th

1648

Our blessed King Charles the first . whose memory shall

live to Eternity was cruelly Murdered, by the hands of

Blasphemous Rebels, his own subjects, at White hall .

London the 30th of January: 1648.

Let all true Christians mourne for the fall of this Stately

Cedar , who, was the chief support of the Church of God.

A holy, Pious, Prince, who fought gods battles a-

-gainst his Enemies. being a nursing father a good

Josiah to his 3 Kingdoms. who for the defence of the true

Catholic Religion. of Jesus Christ his Lord. And for

the defence of the Noble Laws of this Kingdome of England . the

Protestant faith & the Privileges of the Parliament &

Subject. Ruling them in peace & happiness many years.

he laid down his life. beeing sacrificed by the iniquities

of his subjects; Their sins pulled down his Ruin on

him & our selves. let his admirable book speak his

Eternal Glory & praise, the best of Kings (as mere man)

that ever this Earth had. never defiling him self with sin

or blood. of a tender compassionate sweet disposition.

Incomparably Chaste; & free from the least tincture of vice

or Profaneness. Oh how may we take up justly those

bitter lamentations of Jeremy. The Anointed of the Lord

the Joy of our hearts, the light of our Eyes, is taken in their

pits. the Crown is fallen from our Heads. No unto us that we

have sinned, let every soul gird it self with sackcloth, &

lament the displeasure of God which has smitten our head &

wounded the defence of this our English Church our Solomon Hezekiah

in him our staff & stay, is gon. o repent, & humble your selves

you Daughters of Jerusalem, for him that clothed you in Scarlet
