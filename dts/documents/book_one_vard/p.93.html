Upon the beheading of King Charels the Martyr Jan 30th

1648

Our blessed King Charles the first . whose memmory shall

live to Eternity was cruelly Murthered, by the hands of

Blasphemous Rebells, his own subjects, at White hall .

London the 30th of Janueary: 1648.

Let all true Christians mourne for the fall of this Stately

Ceadar, who, was the chief suportt of the Church of God.

A holy, Pious, Prince, who fought gods battailes a-

-gainst his Enimes. being a nursing father a good

Josiah to his 3 Kingdoms. who for the defence of the true

Catholique Religion. of Jesus Christ his Lord. And for

the defence of the Noble Laws of this Kingdome of England . the

Protestant faith & the Privileges of the Parlament &

Subject. Ruling them in peace & happiness many years.

he laid doune his life. beeing sacrificed by the iniquitties

of his subjects; Their sins pulled down his Ruin on

him & our selves. let his admirable book speak his

Eternal Glory & praise, the best of Kings (as meere man)

that ever this Earth had. never defiling him self with sin

or blood. of a tender compasionate sweet disposition.

Incomparabley Chaste; & free from the least tincture of vice

or Profainenesse. Oh how may we take up justly those

bitter lamentations of Jerimie. The Annoynted of the Lord

the Joy of our hearts, the light of our Eyes, is taken in their

pitts. the Crown is fallen from our Heads. No unto us that we

have sinned, let every soul gird it self with saccloth, &

lament the displeasure of God which has smitten our head &

wounded the defence of this our English Church our Solomon Hezekiah

in him our staffe & stay, is gon. o repent, & humble your selves

you Daughters of Jerusalem, for him that clothed you in Scarlett
