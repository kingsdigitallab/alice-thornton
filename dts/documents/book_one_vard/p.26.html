Which were preludiums of this most Excellent

Kings own destruction; when the Commons had got

the Reines of Power, by this in to their own hand

& the better capacitated, to fight against their

Lawfull Soveraine; Albeit they pulled upon them

-selves & the Kingdom a fatall Ruin with in a few

years after, but Principally against our

Sacred Majesty which was the Marke with the Crown

which they Aymed at.

But this galant Earle so much desiring the

Peace & happiness of his Majesty , & Kingdoms

did acquitt the King constrained. & chearfully

submitted to that sentance, with so much Serenity &

tranquility, of thoughts as is immaginable. Only

did justifie his innocency to the death. as maybe

seen in his papers, & last speech. he forgave his

Adversaries, & wished as he was the first that had

laid down his life in this way, for the preservation

of the King & church. so that he might be the last inno

-cent bloodshed. but he much feared it.

He put up prayers for the King , & the whole Kingdom

as it may be seen in his triall. written by an Eye &

eare witness: And upon the 12th of May. 1641
