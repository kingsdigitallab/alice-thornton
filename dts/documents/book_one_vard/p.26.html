Which were preludiums of this most Excellent

Kings own destruction; when the Commons had got

the Reins of Power, by this in to their own hand

& the better capacitated, to fight against their

Lawful Soveraine; Albeit they pulled upon them

-selves & the Kingdom a fatal Ruin with in a few

years after, but Principally against our

Sacred Majesty which was the Mark with the Crown

which they Aimed at.

But this gallant Earl so much desiring the

Peace & happiness of his Majesty , & Kingdoms

did acquit the King constrained. & cheerfully

submitted to that sentence, with so much Serenity &

tranquillity, of thoughts as is imaginable. Only

did justify his innocency to the death. as maybe

seen in his papers, & last speech. he forgave his

Adversaries, & wished as he was the first that had

laid down his life in this way, for the preservation

of the King & church. so that he might be the last inno

-cent bloodshed. but he much feared it.

He put up prayers for the King , & the whole Kingdom

as it may be seen in his trial. written by an Eye &

ear witness: And upon the 12th of May. 1641
