Considering that what ever he doeth is good.

Had he not in much mercy drawne my dear Joy to him

self & fitted him for this dissolution. he remembring the Lord

in the days of his youth & God I hope was found of him.

What although I now doe want those good & Pious prairs

which he put up for me & mine many years enjoyed from him

the benefit of. yet I will trust holy upon the sole mediation and

intercession of our blessed Christ. who ever livs to make in-

tercession for his widow & Orphans. And yet such is my

frailtie. if a deep affection be termed so that I can say

nothing but puts a fresh remembrance. & brings a new flood of

Tears. which I water my Couch with all & widdowed disconsolate

bed, for my self & Children. And yet my thinke I hear

him say as our Saviour to the women that wept after him.

weepe not for me me (that am now in Joy & bless) but weepe for

your self & little ones. I was in the world tormented with

pains. Crosses. losses sickness troubles on every side. But

now I am comforted in the bosome of my Father & thy father

which I longed after: & so shalt thou in his good time.

Is there not then no means to aswage thy immoderate grief

for this sad seperation. O yes. there is hope in the later end.

what although my poor ??? thou art deprived of his presence

doest thou not believe in God. that those Shall be blessed that believe

in him, & by the miritts of his redeemer he now injoyes the

incomprehensable Joys of the great God of Heaven. where all

Tears is wiped off from his eyes. all sorrows is departed from

him. & he is delivered from this Body of Death.
