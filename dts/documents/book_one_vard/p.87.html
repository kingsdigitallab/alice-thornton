Travail she was delivered of a goodly son about Sept 3d by one dame

a sweet child, that died that summer of the small Pox.

This child came double into the world, with such extremity

that she was exceedingly tormented with pains. so that she was

deprived of the benefit of sleep for 14 days; except a few

frightful slumbers, neither could she eat any thing

for her nourishment as usual.

Yet still did she spend her time in discourse of goodness

Excellently Pious, godly & Religious. instructing her

Children, & servants. & preparing her soul for her dear

Redeemer. as it was her saying, She should not be long from

him. That week when I was left with her after my Lady

Armitage , & my Aunt Norton was gon. though she could

not get rest. yet all her discourse was very good & profi--

table to the hearers, who might learn Piety, chastity

holiness, patience, humility, & all, how to entertain the

pleasure of God with contentedness. making so excellent

a confession of faith & other Christian virtues, & graces

that Mr Siddall . exceedingly admired her Parts & Piety

giving her the as high a character as could be.

She did entreat Sir Thomas her husband, to send for Mr

Farrer , & to join with her in the receiving the holy Sacrament

but he would not give leave. which was to my knowledge a

great grief & trouble to her thoughts. That night

she poured out her soul in Prayer with such comprehensive

& good expressions that could be. for her own soul, for pardon

& remission of her sins, for grace & sanctification from the

spirit. faith & assurance, &c. then for her husband Children

Mother and all her other relations. & my self.
