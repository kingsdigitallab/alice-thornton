this generation in righteousness. And at the last may Joy-

heartily beg for the lone sake of our dearest Lord & saviour

Jesus Christ his holy son. Amen & Amen.

A relation of the Last sickness, and Death of my

dear & hon.red husband Will.m Thornton Esquire who

departed this life at Malton Sept. 17th 1668.

While I am in this Vale of Tears, & shadow of Death

I must not desire, nor expect more comforts of this Life

& temporal mercies; then will preserve me from sinking

in sorrow, or despair under the Cross. Even that share was

denied my Saviour the Captain of our Salvation. when

he fainted under it. & almost despaired by the sadder

loads of our Sins. well may I miserable Creature, take

up his Cup & pledge his love with love again. his Life

here had little, or no mixture, but gall & bitterness. I

have the beams of his sweet influences. enjoying some tim

the Sun shine of his favours behind the clouds of despair

& afflictions: fare be it from me to repine at the great &

wise disposer, the Lord of Heaven & Earths most infinitely

wise disposition, or to grudge at his dealing with me. for

here I am Lord make me thine & doe what thou wilt with

me either for life or Death. The Lord best knows how to

propose & intermix Crosses with Comforts. Smiles with frowns.
