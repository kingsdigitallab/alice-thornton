Smith made in his Book for a Person at the point to die

& took great notice of each Petition praying with

Zeal & ardency.

It was very observable in all her sickness, as in-

deed she was not wanting of her gratitude to God

for his exceeding testimonies of his love & mercy

to her in all her preservation. & deliverances of

her & her Children, which she very often repeated

severally enumerated in her best health, So was

it now in her grand weakness & torment, even till

her death. still the subject of her discourse, calling

to mind the wonderful & infinite goodness of God to

her even from her childhood, setting forth his

favours to her Soul: & spiritual mercies innumer-

-able which she particularly mentioned; And then

she mentioned all her manifold preservation, &

deliverances of her person from death, & destruction,

making such an excellent catalogue of all; that it was

a great consolation to the hearers, & proved by these

things, as a great argument of the support of her droo-

-ping spirits now at the hour of death; being a strong

bar of defence against her spiritual adversaries

That God had appeared Gloriously for her that was his

servant, who had delivered her from time to time

when ever she called on him: her Lord never forsaking

her; but brought her to the gates of death in a happy old

Age & to the sight of heaven; where she fain would be

And in him she alone trusted through the merits of Christ

he would still deliver her from hell, & Sin, & Satan, &

Preserve her to his Kingome, there to Live with him for

ever, where she might spend the whole Eternity of Praise

& thanks giving & Hallelujahs of glory to the Blessed Trinity

Till which time she was thirsting, & longing, & desirous to be

dissolved & to be with Christ Jesus her Redeemer.
