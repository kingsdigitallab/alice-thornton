Blessed Portion of Scripture. which our dear Saviour spake

in St Matt. the 11 chap. & 28, 29, 30 verces; which like the

Lightning breakeing in to a darke place; thus it pearced

into the secret darkenesse of my sorrowfull heart & dejected

Soul; bringing refreshment & some hopes of relief & comfort

being increased in the consideration of those heavenly mercies

conteined in that scripture. Lookeing upon this as his sweet

& effectuall call to my soul, wherein he then bid me come

unto him, & alone seeke of him that had strength, power, & mercy

to releive all poor sinful dejected souls, which laboured &

were heavy Laden, with sins the saddest burden then to be borne

Come unto me all yea that labour, & are heavy Laden

and I will give you rest.

Behold the handmaid of the Lord: I laboured under, & was

heavy Laden with the burden of my sins. under the burden of

Satans bondage; & slavery of fears. the one bringing me to

wards dispare, the other of torment, perswadeing that time was

past for any help from God, that the Divill might wreake his antient

malice against souls, on me, in my sickness & weakness

then, being the fittist opportunity to prevail: when he shaks

there faith: Butt Lo in this my distress; the Lion of the

Tribe of Juda put to flight for me, as he had don for him-

selfe, when he tempted him to question his sonship; The

Prince of darkenesse: with this word of his, Come unto me. show

ing thereby that he was a lier. & that it was not to late for me

to repent & believe; nor him to accept of my soul, & so

by this he vanquished his strongest strategem by which he

surpriseth the poor doubting Christian when at the Lowest Ebbe

this he trieth if by any means he can make him doubt or

question the rich goodness that is Etternally in God as his very

being, & so comitt the great sin against his mercy, from

whence flowes mans Salvation. & then he is lost forever.

But at this time, when I was weakest Christ Jesus the Righ

teous came into my heart with healing under his wings

making it apeare that Satan was a lier, & that he never refuseth

any who come unto him; according to that place of: those that

come unto me I will in no wise cast of. He calls, he invites,
