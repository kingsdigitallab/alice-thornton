A bout the 20th day of may: on that night, my dear hus

-band had a dream which he told to my Brother Denton

Mr Comber & my self, in the morning. severally. which

did very much trouble me to hear. That he should

live but 47 days longer: Nor was he ever apt to

take notice of such things, but rather condemn me

for relating several ominous dreams that I had before

the Death of my Father , my Uncle, Osborne , my Sister

Danby , my brother George , before whose death half a

year I dreamt so fully concerning it, the manner & all

circumstances about it. that he refrained that River after

I told him my dream. till that time which was the last &

being compelled by his business to doe it.

Also before my mothers death, & several of my Ch-

-ildrens: So that now he himself was more apt to

make use of this as a warning to him. being often

heard to say he should not live very long. & with much

diligence endeavoured to prepare his soul for God.

In which time I who had my comforts so entirely bou

nd up in him could not with patience almost to think

of this change. If possibly it might be the good pleasure

of the Lord to spare him to us.

we repaired (upon the next illness) to Dr wittie he not

being willing for any others advice. to confided what

course could be taken for his preservation. so that upon

a serious consideration of his distemper, then inclining

to melancholic, he aduised him for the Spa , where he

had bin other years with good success. Upon which Mr Thornton

was very desirous to go, believing it would help him

And The Dr firmly engaged to be as careful of him

as his own Life. so upon the 5th of June: 1667 we

set forward in a Coach with him to Malton . with my 3

Children I thinking to have gon to scarbrough with him

but it so pleased God that I fell exceeding ill that I could

not go. but was forced to return home to newton : &

left him under the care of my Brother Portington & sister
