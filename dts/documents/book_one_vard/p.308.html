I must no more; nor truth could say no less Virtue. & grace, did still thy breast possess. Each muse so plied her Ore, in thee deer Soul Ambitious, which should most in thee, Control. Thy Faith, thy hope, thy Great Charity In lively Colours, shined, bright in thee. Although thy Cruel Enemies with hate: Pursued thy Life, Religion, & thy EState Yet Providence preserved them all, entire. And filled thy breast with holy fire: From that blessed Alter; which on the day before Thou poured thy offerings out, him to adore. And reconciled, thy self, to God; and Man. Prepared for thy great change before it Came What, tho thy death was sudden (to our Eyes). Yet thou being ready (for that Lord) t'was no surpris Thou, changed Earth, for Heaven, foes for friends Then begin thy Joys that Never End: But who can speak the Sorrows that spread oppressed Each Bleeding heart, & Eye and Breast. at this sad object struck with horror Dumb, To see thrice gallant Wansfords mare. mortum ore Run its Banks. That Ancient River Swale By this sad fortune, did a Curse Entail.
