Poor infant with in me was greatly forced with violent

Motions perpetually, till it grew so weak that it had left

stirring, & about the 27th of August, I found myself in

great pains as it were the colic. after which I began to be

in travail. And about the next day at night I was de-

-livered of a goodly daughter ; who lived not so long as

that we could get a minister to baptise it though we

presently sent for one.

This my sweet babe & first Child departed this life half an

hour after its birth. being received, I hope into the Arms

of him that gave it. she was buried that night being friday the

27th of August. 1652. at Easby Church .

The effects of this fever remained by several distempers

successively. first after the Miscarriage. I fell into a most

terrible shaking Ague lasting one quarter of a year. by fits

each day twice in much violency, so that the sweats was great

with faintings. being thereby weakened till I could not stand or go.

After the Ague left me upon a medicine of London Treacle

I fell into the Jaundice,

[word] I.e., jaundice.

which used me very hardly one full

quarter & a half more. I finding Dr wittie s judgment true that

it would prove a cronnicall distemper. but blessed be the

Lord upon great & many means used & all remedies. I

was at length cured of all distempers & weaknesses which

from its beginning had lasted 3 quarter of a year full out.

Thus I had a sad entertainment & beginning of my Change

of life the comforts thereof being turned into much dis-

comforts & weaknesses. but still I was upheld by an All-

mighty Power. therefore will I praise the Lord my God. Amen.
