79

Seeing nothing but a weak company for her Person. &

having lost all in Ireland , only 2 Trunks of wearing

lining. they gave her leave to pass. & about 10 a clock

at night, we came weary into the town of warrington .

After a while, we were entertained, with (Alarms as was

pretended) from the Kings party in Chester . this was

but to awaken their diligence the more. but there was

no cause; for that poor Town had work enough to defend

it self from its Enemies.

From warrington , we went to Wiggen the next day,

being a Town Zealous for their King, & Church. wee

found it sorely demolished, & all the windows broken

many, sad complaints, of the poor inhabitants being

at our first coming was scarred least we should have

bin of the Parlament party. Their cries were the greater in

respect they were enforced, to see the burning of 500d

of their own Bibles. publicly at the Cross by the Soldiers

which they plundered, under pretence of being Popery,

in their Service books. and reviling them with the names

of Papists Dogs. But this Town had bin preserved

from such false doctrine, or heresy, & would have died

for the true Profession of the Protestant religion.

The memory of Dr Fleetewood was so famous at that

time with them, which was a most Pious Godly Minister

living about 30 years since with them & by his life &

doctrine had set such good order amongst them that they

still retained the true Religion he taught. They hearing

that my mother was his niece, flocked abundantly to see her
