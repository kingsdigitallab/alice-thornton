283 I would be fair, but see the Champion Proud The worlds fair Eye, oft setting in a Cloud. I would be wise, but that the Fox I see Suspected guilty, when the Fox is free I would be Poor, but see the humble grasse Trampled upon, by each unworthy Asse. Rich hated; Wise suspected; scornd if poor, Great feared; fair tempted; high still envied more. Would the world then adopt me for her Heir. Would Beauties Queene intitle me the fair. Fame speak me hon.nrs minion, & could I. With Indian Angels, & a speaking Eye. Command baire heads; bowed knees strike Justice Dumb As well as blind & lame, & give a Tongue. To stones by Epitaphs; be calld great Master, In the last lines of every Poetaster: Could I be more then any man that lives. Great, Wise, Rich, Fair, All in Superlatives. Yet I these favours would more free Resigne Then ever fortune would have had them mine. I count one minute of my holy Leasure Beyond the mirth of all this Earthly Pleasure. Wellcome Pure thoughts, wellcome my sadest Groves These are my guests; this is the Joys I love. The winged People of the Skies shall Sing, Me Anthems by my Sellers gentle Spring. A Prayer Book shall be my lookeing glasse Wherein I will adorne Sweet Vertues Face. Here dwells no heartlesse loves no pale fac'd fear No short Joys purchast with Eternal tears Here will I sit & sigh my weak youths folly And learne to affect an holy Mallancholly. And if contentment be a Strainger then. He ne'er look for ill butt in Heaven again. Ah foolish, faithlesse, fickle world where in. Each mottion is a vice, and every act a Sin. finis.
