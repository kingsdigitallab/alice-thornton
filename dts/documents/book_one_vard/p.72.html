Till I grew so dangerously ill, & inwardly sick;

that I was in much peril of my life, by their not coming

well out. but kept at my heart. not withstanding

all the means of Physicians, or others, that my dear Mother

cost & care she used for me. yet I was well nigh death.

But blessed be the most gracious God, & Lord of mercy

, which pleased to hear our petitions for my life, & to spare

me in much mercy. & caused them to come well forth

& so by degrees the malignity of that Disease abated when there

was many in that place died of it.

There was in our house a little boy, that my father had taken

for charity. This Frank Kelly falling sick on good

friday (& I on the next day) was most sadly used in great

extremity, of pain, & sickness, & miserably sore.

& could now swallow. his sight was eaten out, &

his mouth very sore, notwithstanding all the great

care, & industry of my dear mother 2 watchers

& the same helps of a Dr & medicines we both had,

And great was my mothers love & charity for him so that my

dear mother , she did sit up many nights with this

poor boy , & dressed his sores, with all offices as diligently

as if he had bin her own child, notwithstanding

his loathsome disease. All the time of this boys

sickness, he was so full of sweet expressions & Hea

venly minded, with much acts of Religion. that it was a

great comfort to my mother , & all about him. with a

bundance of patience, & gratitude to God, & my mother

for all they had don for him.
