My Mothers going into Ireland 1632.

It pleased God to give my dear Mother , my two

younger brothers & my self a safe passage into

Ireland , about the year. 1632. My Father being

there a year before, & my Eldest brother George

In which place I enjoyed great happiness and

Comfort during my hon.red honoured fathers life having the

fortunate opportunity in that time, & after, when

I stayed there, of the best education that Kingdom

could afford. haveing the advantage of Society in the

sweet & chaste company of the Earle of Strafford s Daughters

The most virtuous Lady Anne . & The Lady Arbella

Wentworth . Learning those qualities with them which

my father ordered. Namely,

The french Language. to write, & speak the same;

Singing. Dancing. Playing on the Lute, & Theorboe.

learning such other accomplishments, of Working

Silks. gummeworke. & Sweetmeats & other

suitable housewifery. As, by my mothers virtuous

provision, & care, she brought me up in what was

fit for her quality, & my fathers Child.

But above all things I accounted it my Chiefest

happiness where in I was trained in those Pious

holy & Religious instructions. Examples, admo-

-nitions.
