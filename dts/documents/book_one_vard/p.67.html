In to England . landing at the Beerehouse neare

Neston . Where we ttarried several weeks by reason of

my distemper, brought out of Ireland , when we fled

from the Rebells.

This I say brought me exceeding weak, so that I had a

Doctor from Chester for my cure. After which with the great

care & love of my dear mother . God was pleased at

that time to restore my life, & strengthen my great

weakness, inabling me to go to Chester in a Coach.

Thus did the great God of heaven, & Earth preserve us

most miraculously in all our dangers & extreamities

bringing us safe, all to our own native Countrey.

Blessed be the most high God, Possessor of Heaven & Earth

which preserved our lives from all manner of destruction.

A thanksgiving for preservation

from the Rebellion in Ireland Octb 23 1641.

O Lord, great wonderful & holy. which sitteth in the

heavens, in the glory of thy majesty far above all things

in heaven & Earth; And that beholdest us the poor sonns

of men with the eyes of mercy & pity when wee are in distress

& calamities, delivering us from Ruin & murders.

What shall I say, or can I express how wee are bound unto

unto thy gracious Majesty for thy Providence that watches

over us for good, who saw & discovered these wicked

bloody practices of our implacable Enemies that secretly

laid traps to destroy our Souls. O how should our gratitud

exceed all others, who has bin delivred by a most strainge

providence & preserved safe sleeping & wakeing.
