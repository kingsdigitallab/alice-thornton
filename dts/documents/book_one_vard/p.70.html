1643

But the warres falling out hott at that time, being wee

were beleagured in Chester by Sir will.m Brewerton s

forces for the Parliament & there happened a strainge

accident which raised that Seige July. 19th 1643

As I was informed; there was 3 granadoes shott in

to the town but through providence hurt noebodie.

The first, being shott into the Sconce of our Souldiers.

with in 2 men of the Captaine Manwaring . but having

an Oxes hide ready, clapt it there on. & it smot-

-hering away in shells did not spread. but went

out. The 2d light, short of the Cittie , in a ditch, with in

a Pasture amongst a company of women milking

but was quenched with out doing them harm at all.

Praised be the Lord our God. The Last fell amongst

their own horse, short of the town . slaing many

of them, & by that means the Seige was Raised.

thus was we freed from great evils to befall that

town, while wee staid there. that sucseeded after

we came into yorkeshire . which still the Lords hand was

Streached out for our Preservation in each place

we came to it was a sanctuary to us, blessed be the Lord

most hight for all his goodness towards us:

But I had in this time of the seige, a grand deliverance
