My Cozen John Norton died of a consumption, long in

A languishing condition, but at length it pleased God

to take him to himself in the year 1646.

The Death of Sir Edward Osborne . 1646.

My uncle. Sir Edward Osborne who was my mothers

owne half brother; was a very good, wise & prudent, man

under whose tuition my father left the hope of his house

my brother George , as being joint guardian with my Mother

he had so fraternal a love for & parental care over my

Dear mother, & us all; that we were most happy in

him. & during his life, this our Family was kept in much

peace & tranquillity, he seeing that each party had its

right & dues. with a care for the due observance of my

fathers will, of which he was an Executor

Butt after his death, we (that is to say) my Mother , & her

Children was much oppressed, & injured, through the bad

Menagerie of all that Estate, & that was all seized on by my

Uncle Wand. for the Debts, which he was much wronged of too,

by one he made a Lease of it for 7 years, taking many

100d pounds more then his due, & before he got it again

he put him to a suite. but in this time all the Children was

Maintained, by my dear Mother , from her Jointure.
