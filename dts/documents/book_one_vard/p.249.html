Neither could I be informed of such slanders against

me till by Mrs Danby s maides taking an occasion to fall

out with me about the dressing the Chamber when it was very

foule: she being in that womans place which they pretended

had first raised those lies; And desiring to have bin in my

own maides place; sought an occasion to undermine her

of that place; or else to give me warning to provide for one

in hers: having a desire to be at liberty; which I was not un

willing to in regard of my self: but because I kept her with

me till my Nece Danby was resettled in her own house, &

then she was to go with her. & to have don Mrs Danby a

kindness in it: But on the contrary; it proved an infortu

-nate act. by reason of their disgusts both of the mistresse &

servant against my self & family. yet it was the occa-

-sion that brought these misteries of forgeries to my knowledge

The maid telling me that she could tell me who had abused

& wronged me in my Family; but not unlesse she went a-

way for making a mischeife as she called it.

Upon which words I told her; that I should have bin beholden to

her if she knew I was wronged, & to let me know by whom

but she said, no not unlesse she went a way. I said it was

very hard if I could not be informed of the injury was don with out

she went away: & I would be determined by her mistresse .

So finding her mistresse in the same humour having agreed to

gether that I should not be righted by them unlesse she went a

way. it troubled me much to see such dealing. as also that I

was abused, as was pretended by others; when as she which

was my friend should had; consealed the matter, to make her

own advantage against me. Nor should I have bin told

any thing to have righted my self at all. if her maids

quaraling unjustly had not produced this effect.

What was the cause of these proseedings. God knowes for I

had not given either of them just occasion whom I had treated

like sisters in my house, but it was disernable. that some

sinister end was at the botome & not out of Love to the truth

or me that things was thus ordered.
