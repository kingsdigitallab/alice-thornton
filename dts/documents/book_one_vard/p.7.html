A Preface to the book.

For as much as it is the dutie of every true Christian

to remember, & take notice of Allmighty God our,

Heavenly Fathers, gracious Acts, of Providences,

over them, & merciful dealings with them, even from

the womb, untill the Grave burie them in silence, As

also to keep particular remembrances of his favours

both Spiritual, & temporal, together, with his remarkable

deliverances of their Souls & Bodies with a true and

unfeined gratitude to his Glorious Majesty for them all.

I therefore his Creature, & unworthy handmaid.

who have not tasted (only) of the dropping of his dew;

but has bin showred plentifully upon, my head, with

the continued Streames of goodness, Doe most humbly

desire to furnish my heart with the deep thoughts

& apprehensions, & sincere meditations of and

thankefullnesse, For his free Grace, love, mercies &

inconceavable goodness to me his poor Creature.

Even from them first beginning.

And with a most Cordiall, & sincere heart thankefully

doe return him the Glory of all. First,

For my Birth & baptizme in the name of the most holy

Trinitye: my strict Education in that true faith of the
