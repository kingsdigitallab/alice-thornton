68

When as thousand souls, more innocent then our selves

perished. & was swallowed up sudainly in the pitt of

horrid murder & fury prepared also for us in Dublin

Oh my God what shall I say, which am astonished at

the miracle which none but thine Almighty all seeing

powes, & hand, could have prevented. thou O Lord

hadst a care of me even from my birth, & youth up.

And hast preserved me from great &, infinite dangers

but this exceeds all other thy boundlesse goodness to

my self. My mother , & brothers with her two grand-

chilldren ; from the utter destruction of all her Posterity.

O what shall I render to the Lord our God for this his great

deliverance of all our Souls. That did not give us up to

destruction amongst the many 1000ds that perished. to be

murdered, stript, slaine, burned, drowned, or into

any one of their damnable practtices against my

innocent Soul. O Lord what am I, or my fathers

family, that thou shouldest take care of us.

But even for thine own name sake, & tender mercy

who gave us our lives for a Prey, in a strainge Place

and delivered not up our lives to the sword, famine

Pestilence, & Rebellions. I will magnify the name of

the Lord most high; while I have my being will I praise

thy holynesse for ever. which saved me from perishing

in the sea. and gave us a speedy & safe passage out

of Ireland , free from stormes, & tempests. Raising

me up from death, that I was nigh unto, even of that flux

he it was that brought me helps, & blessed the means which

was given me to cure that infirmity.
