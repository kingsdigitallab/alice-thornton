widow the summe of 300 per Annum -which never received any part

thereof. yet notwithstanding all the aforesaid goodnes

of hers to that Estate; there wanted not some who putt

hard thoughts into the Heires mind, that she dealt hard

by because she did not give all her widdowes Patrimony

to him, when as all her former helpes did redound

to his benefit, & with all they knew our Estate was more

burthened at that time; which might require such

helpes from her, because we were contented for the

Ease of Kirklington , to receive my Portion from thence

yearely & not in an Intire Summe;

Besides this she fitted my youngest Brother John W.

with the opportunity of good Schooles, as Beedall . Chester ,

Richmond , with all other provissions, of maintenance &

Bookes & all necessarys; for Cambridge, leaveing him

under the Tuittion of Dr widdrington in Christs Colledge

maintaining him there all the time, which by reason of

a sore feavour that seized on him there, he cost her after

the Rate of above 100l per Annum.

All which time his Anuity Lay dormant in the Estate of my

father , which I supose was some advantage to the Heir.

In fine, great & many were the good & Charitable Acts

this most dear & Excellent mother of ours did to us

all: she so wisely & justly disposeing her Estate

Amongst us: that none had the least cause to complaine

But bless God for her wise dispensation.

Being truly thankful for the safe protection care &

preservation we enjoyed under her wing.
