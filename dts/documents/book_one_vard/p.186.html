My greatt Sickness at oswoldkirke february 13th 1661

I began a dangerous Sickness, which brought me very nigh

to Death; at oswoldkirke after my dear mothers death.

which was caused by that Cold I got. & Aguish Temper on the

Sunday in the Church; causing a very violent vomitting

coming greatly upon Shrove Tuesday. feb. 13 1661 being

that day my birth day.

And so contineueing perpetually in pain & vomitting

till I was not able to receive any kinde of Sustenance.

being then fallen into an Intermitting feavour. So that at

5 days end, I was compelled to send for Dr wittie .

He saw cause to let me blood, giving me many Cordialls

to strengthen the stomacke; but noething would stay with me.

Till I dranke a draught of cold water, which more refreshed

my Thirsty Soul; then all what Art could give:

That night being the 17th of feb. I did veriely believe, sho

should be my last in this Life; I being brought into an

Exceeding weakness & feeblenesse of Body & spirit.

But most of all into a seeming Spiritual dessertion

from God: which was not to be long endured. with out sinking

under the Pressures of the divine displeasure. Which I apprehe-

-nded in this my weakness. my Eternal State to be in a

Lost condition, by reason of my Sins; And Satans

Accusing me falsely from my sorrows, & temporal sufferings

Casting multitude of doubtings into my heart. As that

Surely I did not belong to God. And I was not his Servant

because he followed me with such crosses, Afflicktions, sicknes

& troubles in this world; which seemed to be Curses; rather then

such trials, as he used to lay upon those that were his Children.

And that I had neglected, al those offers, & tenders of mercy

in his word & Sacraments, which he from time to time, had given

me from my youth up. in the examples, instructions & admoniti

ons from my dear Parents , & from his, ministers; All his

great deliverances. & checks, & calls of his spirit. with his

spirittuall & temporal preservations of me innumerable.

(yet said Satan) All these had not wrought a through conver
