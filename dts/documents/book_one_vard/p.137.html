Mercy I conceaved of another child having somewhat

a better time in breeding it and did fully intend by Gods

blessing to nurse it my self if I had strength;

About a week before my full time I continued in much

pain through the heavinesse of my Child ; having the mid-

-wife in constant expectation each hour. at which time

my dear mother laid sick in the house of a sad cough. &

yong Mr Troutbeck came to give her directions through

some friends advice. & by the blessing of God she was recov

-ered though not to be with me in my travell. all teusday

& that night with the morning on wednesday I was in great ex-

-treamity till I was delivered. which came to pass on wednesday

the 14th of february. half hour after 11 a clock in the forenoone

of a very sweet goodly daughter, & a delicate child, for

which most gracious mercy in my deliverance with my infants

I render most faithful & infinite thanks to the great & mighty

God of heaven & earth which had compassion on me in my extrea

-mity.

After I was delivered & in bed at 9 a clock at night.

it fell out that my little daughter naly , then newly weaned

& being asleepe in the cradle fell into a desperate fit of

the convoltions, as it was suposed to be, her breath stoping

& grew blackish in her face. which did sore afright her maid

Jane Flouer , who immediately took her up & with Jane

Rimer the midwife made help to recover her life. but all

that night she was so exceeding weak that my Aunt Norton

satt with her & many others expecting when she would have

died.

During this poor childes illnesse, I was almost at deaths

dore my self. by a great Ilnes coming after I was in bed

so that my Aunt & friends did immagine I could not live

nor durst they tell me how weak my sweete Naly was at

that time least griefe should have dispatched me hence.

but they removed her in the cradle into the Parlour.
