Tormented, us getting all our provisions of meat and drink

, let us want all necessaries, yet there domineering & in

sulting voluptuousness must be supplied, & my mother

was charged for 18 or 20 months together with 25l a month

in monies to the Soldiers, besides the quartering of a troop

of Scots on free quarter, which was treble the value of

her Estate. & at that time she borrowed monies to

maintain all her 4 children my brot. Christo . & John were at Beedale scoole from nov. the 16, 1643. many years which she paid afterwards

Albeit we had a particular maintenance to have bin

paid out of Kirklington , & for the heir's part out of Hud-

-swell . Yet even in these times most sad and lamentable

did the Lord most high preserve us from ruin, utterly &

made us have a Place of safety under his wings of

Protection. all those Evil times of fears and distractions

Blessed & praised be the god of our Salvation. Amen.

The death of my Sister Danby : September 30th 1645 Sept. 10th 1645

at her house at Thorpe .

About this year my deare, & only Sister the Lady

Danby drew near her time for delivery of her 16th Child

Ten whereof had bin baptised. the other 6. were still

borne, when she was above half gon with them, she have

-ing miscarried of them, all upon frights by fire in her

Chamber, falls, & such like accidents happening. All her

Children were Sons. saving my two nieces, Katherine &

Alice Danby , & most sweet, beautiful, & comely were they

all. The troubles, & distractions of those sad times

did much afflict & grieve her, who was of a Tender &

sweet disposition, wanting the company of her husband

Sir Thomas to manage his Estate. & other concerns.
