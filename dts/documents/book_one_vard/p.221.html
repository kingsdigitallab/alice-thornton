Praied to his God & heartily recomended his Soul to

his Creator. and so laied him self quietly down: he

drew his breath shorter each minuitt & at last sweetely fell a

sleep in the Lord. thus was the conclusion of that dear, &

sweet brother. so much afflicted & under so long a

weakness. his condition was much to be lamented. & it

was indeed a contineuall grief, & daily corrosive to my

self, whose nearenesse in affection & consanguinity had

a sufficient simpathy in his sufferings.

He was a very great loss to our Family, by his trouble

& visittation; whose parts. Piety, Learning. & quick witt

was not infeariour to any that was remaining of it. being

of a most sweet, affable, & injenious nature. nimble &

ready in his acqute answers in disputes. as well at

Skoole as Cambridge . At both places he carried him self

so obleigingly that all Persons of his acquaintance loved & admired in his sigaci

tie coming nearest to his bro. George & for witt & Parts.

my dear mother had given him the best education she could

sending him to Cambrige about 15 years old: under the

Tuittion of Dr widdrington where he made great im-

provement of his time in the sciences, & learning. & was

so Studious. even at nights. & when his hours should have

bin for recreation: that his Tutor was forced to forbid that sever-

rity; his Sabaths were spent allwais (as much as he could)

very strict, in acts of Religion & Piety, Truly from his

childhoode & youth studing to serve & fear God.

In fine he was so hopefully good & pregnant, that my mot.

had much comfort & all his relations joy in him. being

full of expectations that this early plant might flourish to

the hon.or & happiness of the whole family. But it so hapned.

to our great grief; that this malancoly seising on him first at

Cambrige in a great sickness. then he was by an infinite

care & cost of my mother , he was perfectly well again. but

upon the former accidents mentioned, he fell very ill.

& yet all her care was contineud over him even at London

where he had bin inticed to go from her contrary to her mind
