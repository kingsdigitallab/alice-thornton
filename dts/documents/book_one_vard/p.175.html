Which she immediately did to all their great Comforts

for she lifted up both her Eyes & hands steadfastly

to heaven three times, distinctly one after another.

And closing her Eyes her self, then laid down her

head & her Eyes: this holy saint , & Matrone of true

Piety sweetly fell a sleep in the Lord. between the hours

of 8 & 9. a clock at night upon Saturday the 10th of

December: 1659 being the day of her Coronation I

hope in heaven, with her Father, Receiving that welcome of

Come the blessed of my Father, receive the Kingdom he has

Prepared for you.

For I was an hungry & yea fed me, naked & yea clothed

me, Sick & imprisoned & yea ministered unto me.

In as much as yea did it unto these yea did it unto me

And I hope she is now Entered into the Joy of her Lord:

My Brother Christopher wandesforde was then at London

where he was writ to inform him, both of her Sickness

& death. Her funeral was solemnised with as much

handsomeness as those times would afford & considering

the condition wee were in the soldiers having bin quartered

amongst us. Though not according to her worth & quality.

She was interred upon Tuesday the 13th of December following

In the Chief Place in her own quire at Cattericke Church

She having Repaired the same all that summer at her

own Charges to the value of above twenty Pounds.

Her Corpse was carried out of her house by the Lord

D'arcy . his son Coll. D'arcy . Sir Christoph. Vivill Baronet.

& divers other Persons. & kindred of quality.

Then from Hipswell greene . her Tenants. took her; &

so carrying her to the Town of Catterick . where the ministers

who was appointed by her own nomination Carried

her into the Church ; & after Sermon laid her in her Gave

[word] I.e., grave.

The ministers names were these. Mr Petter Sammois ; Mr

Kirton , Mr Ferrers ; Mr Edrington , Mr Bindows , Mr Robinson

Mr Smith , Mr Brockell . Mr Parke . Infinite numbers of

Poor were served by dole at the door above 1500 besides in the

Church of Cattericke .
