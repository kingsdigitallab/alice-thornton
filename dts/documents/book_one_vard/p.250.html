When I perceavd my self thus usued: rather then I

could indure to lie under wrongs, and of what nature I

knew not; nor how to come to understand them I told them

that Barbara might doe as she pleased. if she did not like

my service she was free to please her self. though I could

bin glad she would stay while her mistris stayed. Upon this

she was resolved to go away. but I earnestly desired

her mistres would give her leave to tell me. how I was ab

used & by whom. that I might not still continue so. & I

should have bin obleiged to her if she would have told me

of it her self upon the account of freindship.

But Mrs Danby carried so infinitly high & strangly

because her maid was to go from her that I got nothing

but scornes upon my intreaties, which much amazed me:

Although she gave me possitive warning to provide telling

me she would not stay any longer. To what hight then was

these persons comed: who did not sticke to affirme I turnd

her off. & not she me. which I have witness of. but to lay it on me

Munday the 20th of July was the day apointed upon my

intreaty to be informed by them of my wrongs: which indeed

had near, have bin my last in this life falling out of

my perfect health, into a very dangerous sickness, on

the occasion of sudden & violent grief & terror, which

I was seized upon, in Mrs Danby s Chamber at Newton

being most straingly surprised at Barbara s accusation

before my face of my servant Hanna Ableson for telling

her several stories from another servant , very great

lies & fallshoods against my self. of such a nature as

I did abhorre & much unbecoming any to hear & not to

haue acquainted me with at first that I might have brought

that person to condigne punisment as Mr Thornton would

have don, if it had bin from her it was laid on: but it

was doubted to be of new invention to have outed this

maid of my favour: who when she heard those horrid

things. did utterly deny them all & cleared her self on

oath; that she never knew nor heard any thing from me
