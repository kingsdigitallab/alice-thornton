The death of my Brother John Wandesford: De. 2d 1666.

It pleased God to vissitt my deare Brother John Wand.

At London . with his last sickness, being an Ague: joined

with violent fits of the stone. having had several of them

the last half year; his sickness continued so strongly

that he was very weak in body. though I bless God perfect

in mind & spirit. I am the better able to show the same from

the relation of his own servant, & the People where he lived

Testifieing this truth to Mr Comber . who was then at London

& intreated by me to vissitt my dear brother in his affliction

at the newes of hearing of me he did much rejoice & faine

would have seen his dear & beloved sister. but when he

understood I was in Yorkeshire . he praied them to remember

his dear love to me. & thanke me for sending to see him.

with abundance of Testimonys of his affection & love to me &

mine praing for us. then did he more Zealously prepaire for

his change & departure out of this miserable world. God have

-ing givn him a taste of heaven & happiness.
