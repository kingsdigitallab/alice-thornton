London 1631.

The first dawning of Gods Spirit in my heart.

After this, it pleased God to come into my Soul

by some beams of his mercy in putting good thoug-

-hts into my mind, to consider, his Great & miraculous

Power, in the Creation of the Heavens, the Earth, & all

the Hosts of them. I was moved to this meditation

upon the reading of the daily Psalms for the months

happening that day to be Psal. 147: v. 4th

He counteth the stars & calleth them all by their

Names. From whence there came into my heart

a forcible consideration, of the Incomprehensible

Power, & infinite Majesty of Almighty God, who

by his Wisdom made all things, in Heaven & Earth.

being above all his Creatures in the world.

knowing what is in man, & searching, all their

ways. Seeing my heart & thoughts, & knew also that

I was but a child both in Age & understanding.

And not able to doe any thing that was good. which struck

me into a deep fear, & great awe of his Glorious

Majesty. least I should offend him at any time by

sin. against him or my Parents . & he would punish all

Sinness; were they never so many, as the Stars, yet

he was as well able to keep an account, & finish for them

as to tell the Stars & give them names.

This, with other, the like meditations of his omnipotence.
