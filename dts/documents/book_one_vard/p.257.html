& chastestest woman in the world: & could not prevail with all

her threats against her. When my Aunt perceived her malice

she told her that she did not deserve what I had don for her in

all things which she had heard from her self: And although she her

self did witness me to be wronged. it was a sign she would

fain have some occasion against me were it never so unjust

And after wards: upon her telling her that my husband desired

she should prepare her self to go from hence. & that I would

procure her a coach if she pleased to go any whither amongst

her friends: Upon which she said I promised to keep. her & she

would not go out of this house. But my Aunt told her if

I did so. the more unworthy wretch was she that had so abused

me, & to this purpose (the innocentest & faithful friend she had.)

At length my good Aunt so ordered the business that she named

a day for her going; & there was a coach procured from Madam

Grahme : my noble friend; which carried her to Yorke with 2

servants to wait on her. being on friday the 11th of sept. 68.

At her departure I gave her to supply her necessities 3l more

to the former 5l sent to her: she having had out of my Purse

the year about 20l in all with charges about her Journeys, &

all supplies of Linen & other necessaries.

I was exceeding weak in bed when she went. nor did I hope to

recover either health or strength again had it not bin out of the

infinite Riches & goodness of my gracious God; which wrought

miracles on me his faithful innocent handmaid.
