To carrie us home to Dublin where my dear Mother

was received with all Joy & gladnesse. living in

much peace & happiness till the death of my Hon.red

Father . When about a year after his death. she

was forced to flie into England , upon the Rebellion

which brake out in Ireland .

But I must not forget a second preservation of

my own Self from drowneing. out of that Ship, at

that time, when a cable from the bote that came to

carrie us to land, & being tied to our Ship. by it's

force, had like to have pulld me out of the Ship in

to the Sea. Which it had don, but that by Gods providence

a Shipman coming at that minuite from under the

Decke pulled me back, & saved me from falling

into that Tempestious Sea , when I was half over

borde. This great & wonderfull mercy, & my Single

deliverance must not be forgotten but had in a

perpetuall remembrance of me forever & therefore

will I humbly set forth the mercies of the Lord to me.

A thanksgiving for our deliverances.

after our dangerous passage by Sea:

August the 23d 1639. into Ireland .

Psallme 107. 1st v.
