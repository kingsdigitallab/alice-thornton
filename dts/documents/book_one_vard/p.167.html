Often would she say; that the way to Heaven was by the

gates of hell, And that the Lion of the Tribe of Judah would

deliver her. likewise would she frequently break

out & say with the sweet Psalmist of Israel, in the mid-

st of her inexpressible pains & torments. Why art

thou so full of heaviness, oh my Soul, & why art

thou so disquietted within me: I will still hope in

my God, & putt my trust in the God of my salvation

who is the help of my countenance and my God,

She frequently repeated the 71 Psalm, which she said

was penned for old age. Surely she was a great exam-

-ple & pattern of Piety, faith, patience, of fortitude

& Resolution to with stand all the fiery darts of Satan

which he in her weakness cast to affright, & hinder

her Journey to Heaven. but he in whom she putt her

whole confidence. & served from her youth up. did

not now leave her in extremity, but so assisted her

in soul & spirit that it was. an heavenly sight to the

beholders even to her last Period, & not withstanding

all her torments, still she put forth her self for the

Glory of God, & the good of her family & beholders

In good instructions, severe reproofs for all sins in

general with a continual praying to god & praising

him in Psalms suitable for her condition, speaking

to God in his own phraise & word, saeing that we could

not speak to him from our selves, in such an accapt-

-able a manner as by that which was dictated by his own

most holy Spirit. She often desired her friends to

pray with her, & for her, & told them that she desired that

they would not pray for her continuance in this life

for she was weary of it & desired to obtain a better

& to be fitted for it; And that these should be the heads

on which they should Petition God for her Videlz.
