After his dressing in the morning: so that I perceived him

not to be well; upon which I gave him Gascon's Powder

And having had 3 hours sleep his face when he a-

waked was full of Red round spots like the Small Pox

being of the compass of an halfpenny; & all wealed white

over. These continuing in his face till night.

but then, whether through cold upon his dressing

then, or what else was the cause; the Lord knoweth. the

spots struck in, and grew very sick all night. and

about 9 a clock on saturday morning. he sweetly

departed this life, to the great discomfort of his weak

Mother; whose only comfort, is that the Lord I hope has

Received him to that place of rest in Heaven, where little

Children, beholds the face of their heavenly Father,

to his God, & my God; whom I humbly crave to pardon

all things in me which he sees amiss, & cleanse away my

sins by the blood of my dearest Saviour. & Redeemer

And that my soul may be bettered; by all these chastise

-ments he pleaseth to lay upon me his vile worm. &

unprofitable servant under all his dispensations. That

hath laid heavy upon me for these many years, Where

by he has corrected me; but not given me over to death

& destruction. for which I humbly magnify his Glorious

Name for ever. And I most heartily beseech him to san-

-ctifie these fatherly rebukes, & make them profitable

to my poor Soul to bring me in the possession of Patience

nearer to him self by a strict communion to see him with

Joy above all this Earthy Comforts or enjoyments. That so

I may be better prepared for acting to his Glory here &

here after; Even for Christ Jesus his son's sake. Amen

My Son will.m Thornton was buried at Easby in the

Same grave with his Eldest Sister which died before baptism

by Mr Kirton . he being scarce fourteen days old.

his father being much troubled at his loss whom the Child

was Exceeding like in Person & also his Eldest Sister.
