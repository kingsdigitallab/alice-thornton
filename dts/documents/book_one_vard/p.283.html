My faithful Soul's wish.

Oh; had I of his love but Part, That chosen was, by Gods own heart. That Princely Prophett David he, Whom in the word of truth I see. The King of Heaven so dearely loved, As mercy beyond measure proved: Then should I neither Gyant fear, Nor Lyon that my Soul would teare. Nor the Philistines, nor such friends. As never, were true Christians friends. No passions should my Spirit vexe Nor sorrow so my mind perplex. But I should still all glory give Unto my God by whom I live. And to the Glory of his Name Through out the world divulge the same. My walk should be but in his ways My talke but onely in his Praise. My Life a death, but in his love. My death a life for him to prove. My care to keep a Conscience cleane. My will from vaine thoughts to waine. My pain, and pleasure, travell, ease. My God thus in all things to please Nor Earth, nor Heaven should me move, But still my Lord should be my love If I am sick he is my health. If I am poor. he is my wealth, If I am weak, he is my strength, If dead, he is my Life at length. If scorne'd, he only is my Grace; If banished, he my Resting place.
