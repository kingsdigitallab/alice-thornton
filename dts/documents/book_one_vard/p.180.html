A thanksgiving for the restoration of King Charles

the second . of his Coronation May. 29th 1660.

After the Lord had taken my Child from me. I had

some weakness upon my body by reason of the return

of my Milk, but in much mercy I was restored to

a pretty degree of strength. & staing at St Nickolas

till I was perfectly recovered.

In this time we had that grand blesing to the whole

Nation given to us in the restoration of our Dread Sover

aine Lord King Charles . when we each moment feared

Ruin & destruction. being in that concern, so wisely

& prudently ordered through the Providence of God that

notwithstanding, the opposittion of the Soldiery & other

great factions; yet was he brought in, to his own

Kingdom; City of London , & Parliament in great Peace

& exceding much Joy from all parts of the Kingdom

withoute the power of one dogge that durst open his mouth, or the

loss of one drope of blood shed in the whole Kingdme.
