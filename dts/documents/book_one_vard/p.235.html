And in the company of Dr wittie . the 8th of June following he went

by a hired coach to scarbrough . And about a month after

he returned home on horse back. it being his own desire:

All which time we had a dear & comfortable corrospondancy

betweene us, by our mutuall And frequent letters. I heareing

of him, or from him 3 times in a week & ofter

[word] I.e., oftener?

each one bring

-ing me the good newes of his having cast off his malancoly

It cannot be immagined, what my Joy was in his return

to finde him so prettily recruited by a chearefull temper

of spirit. but I quickly saw: that his weakness had left

much dreggs of his distemper. yet had I great cause

of thankefullnesse to the Lord which gave me hopes of him.

& that if he could have bin prevented of cold he would remaine

well.

|On Mrs Anne Danby s going to Hooly. Ap. 20 67.

Servant to the young Countesse of sussex ; and accounts

by her to be the dearest friend she had in the world) of her

hard usuage by Madam Danby ; & several discontents

framed by her servant in my house , adding to her for-

-mer disgust towards me in her being cast out as she

said for my sake. these, with other secret unjust reasons

of her own. bey these 2 persons was a mutuall compliance

in there designs & Mrs Danby s underming me in secret

brought Mrs Batte late at night to my house at newton

under pretence of providing butter for Mrs Danby at the

service at hooly to waite on that mad Countesse of Sussex

Mrs Batt told me. she came to fech her cosen danby a

away. & that she would have her from me. I upon this first

salutation being surpreised with her discourse as well as her

again that I had bin desirous to see her & of her acquaintance
