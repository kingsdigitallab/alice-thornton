Many such like expressions I have heard him

my self, then would he call on me, to his bed

side, & stediely lookeing on me, would sighe

& say. Ah poor child, what must thou see

& thine Eyes beholde. And praing for me,

turned away with a great grone.

Which expressions stucke so deep. that I never for

-gate them, but has sadly experienied those

miseries which he prophetically foresaw.

The Bishop of Derrey being called on thursday at

night, who told him he perceived he grew weaker

in body, that he would doe well, to declaire in

public his faith, & hope in God. not that he qu-

-estioned the same, he being fully satisfied.

but that it was usuall in those cases; for the comfort

& instruction of the companie.

Immeadiately my dear Father raised up

him self with all his force. And stedfastly fixe-

ed his Eyes to Heaven. then made before many

Persons of qualitie (with my mother .) A most

Heavenly, & Pathetically confession of his faith

hope, & confidence in God. And that his heart did

fully relie upon the all saveing mirritts of Jesus

Christ his Redeemer. in him alone hoped for pardon

&, remission of all his Sins, & for Salvation through

his blood which was shed for him.
