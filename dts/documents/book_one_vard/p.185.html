In this time while we were at Oswoldkirke . My brother

Denton Preached there. & did that with much gravity & Piety

being indeed a very excellent good. & wise man from whom all

our Family had received many assistances & helpes from

by way of advice; & cheifely my Husband , who depended

much on his counsel after our coming thither.

But he having some scruples, did not conforme to the now

Established Government of the Church; so that he did leave

that Church after a while. & retired to live with us at

Newton . I confesse his nonconformity did much trouble me

& I endeavoured with my small mite to discourse that bussinese

but I supose he had bin otherwise ordeined then Episcopall

so that it was fruitlesse to perswade,) otherwaies, it might

have bin better for us all. though he was ever of a quiett &

peaceable temper; free from faction, or disturbances of the

state: And indeed I enjoyed much comfort in his ministery

And great Assistance, as toward the building of our house at

Newton ; Mr Thornton not giving himself to take pleasure

or trouble in any thing of that nature: being much addicted

to a Melancolicke humour; which had seized on him by fits

for several years before he was 20 years old.

Neverthelesse; after the use of great means. which God directed

Me in by Dr wittie &c. as Leaches & gentle course of Phisick

Spring & fall as there was occasion. ever since we were

Married; those were much abated. & he offtener was in a

more chearefull frame of spirit then formerly. as both he

& his friends. has often times acknowledged. to my great

comfort: whose uttmost endeavours. & care, was ever to

study his good & satisfaction. in whose life & wellfaire my

own did much subsistt. yet after his house was don & we

in it he took much content in itt. when he was well.

In this time while I lived there, about Shrove Sunday 61

I was in the Church when it was a frost & snow, sitting in the minis

Pews. I fellt my self exceeding Cold. & by fits contineud till the

tusday following. very ill:
