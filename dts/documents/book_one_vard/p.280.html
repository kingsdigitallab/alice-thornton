281 But what although thy Lord is gon. To sit in Glory, placed on his Throne. Has he not left his pledge of love. To thee his Loyal Spouse, his holy Dove. Bequeathed Thee his Sanctifying Spirit For to conduct thy weary steps to inherit. Those Everlasting Joys he has prepared. For thee. A Glorious Tabernacles Shared. Wherein no Sun needs Shine, for he alone. Is all the Light in that vast Horizon. What then if through a Sea of briny Tears Thou swime'st, he'll free thee from all fears. Of Sinking, canst thou but hold him fast In Arms of faith, thou shalt come safe at last Nay weart thou dead, yet shalt thou live A Life much more Superlative. Then heart can think, or tongue can tell Those Glories all, they doth Excel. He strive till Death; but shall my feeble strife Be Crowned. He Crown thee with a Crown of Life.

Against the fears of Death.

Since natures works be good and Death doth serve As natures work. why should we fear to die. Since fear is vain, but when it may preserve. Why should we fear, that which we cannot fly. Fear is more pain than is the pain. it fears. Disarming humaine minds of Native might; While each conceit an ugly figure bears Which were not Evil, well viewed in reasons light Our only Eyes, which dimmed, with Passions be, And scarce discern the dawn of coming day. Let them be cleared, and now begin to see, Our life is but a step in dusty way. Then let us hold, the bliss of gracious mind. Since this wee feel. great loss we cannot find.

O Death I will be thy Death. O Grave I will be thy

Victory. thanks be to God: through our Lord Jesus Christ.
