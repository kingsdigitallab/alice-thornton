so they drank on, till the Protestant was forced to require

leave to go into the next room, for they durst not trust

him farther. And he withdrawing thither. broke down

the window, & leaped out of an upper room. & over a

wall before he could make an Escape to acquaint the

Lord Justices. But this man had also the River to Swim

at that time of night which was 12 a clock before he came

to the first Justice which was Sir Will.m Persons .

Rising come to the Gate he was forced to threaten hard

before he was admitted. & then he told him. My Lord

I am sent to you by the Providence of God. to save your

Life & all the English. I am bound in conscience to deliver

my own soul in there preservation from the Irish who

intends to destroy them all, & told him all the foregoeing

circumstances telling him with all that he must not look on

him as an idle drunken fellow. but as one which had all

-most lost his own life to preserve his. & that if he did

not take care to prevent this mischief all the innocent

blood of the English would be required at his hand.

With many such like expressions. after which there was search

made for the two Rebels . but non was found till the same man

which discovered the Plot found them himself, hid in the top of

that house with in a Trap door. so they were taken & secured

& we all poor sheep destinated to destruction was thus

wonderfully preserved & delivered in Dublin .
