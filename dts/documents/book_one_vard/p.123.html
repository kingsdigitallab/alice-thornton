After which agreements Articles & writtings done, there

followed a pretty space, ere his Mother had passed a

fine with him, in regard that his Father in Law , would not

joyne in the fine. & my Mothers counsel did not approve

of it to be legall for security with out it. but Mr Thorton

did faithfully ingaged to doe the same leagally &

pass the fine after his death.

but in the intrime I was left in an incertainty for the

security of a Jointure in case of his death before me, &

when my Portion was disposed of by him, could have

no benefit thereby. so that my Mother could proceed no

further, least any ill consequent might follow, but

wholely this business was Left to my own choyce, what

I would doe in this case. she being loth as upon her

own account, to under go such a concern in which

there was such a hazard. wherein she was not to be blamed,

butt Mr Thornton was very much troubled upon this un-

just deniall of his Father in Law . by which he was likely

to have undon his desires. fell into much sadnesse &

discontent, which perhaps might hasten his Sickness that

he fell into, on his going from hipwell.

his Ague began in the way, to his mothers when finding

himself not able to go through. came back to Hipwell

falling exceeding ill into a fever, But upon the advice

of Dr wittie . he was let blood, & had all means used for

his recovery. but was brought dangerously ill. at the last

it pleased God that he recovered beyond all expectation.

And upon his recovery, I was willing to relie upon his

promise to my Mother , & his infinite expressions to my self I

rested upon Providence in the fullfilling of his desire. in

so much that about the December following proceeded our marriage

this is the true relation of this grand concern of my life, which

I have bin the more teadious in, because I would leave to

Posterity the Right understanding of that concern.
