So that upon consideration of the whole business; I have

mentioned. It was conseaved, convenient to entertaine

this motion & to close with such a happy oppertunity which

by the Providence of God was directed. And by reason of

her youth we resolved to keep it secret till a fitter time

when this intended business should be consumated.

There being still an affectionate corrospondancy of these

Persons by Chaste & religious conversations in our Family.

Neither was I out of hopes that Sir Christopher Wandesforde

might duely pay his 2000l out of Ireland which was to

redeeme that Estate & pay Mr Thornton s Ingagments.

But in the meane time. I might more probably die in some

of these sicknesses & my dear husband . who was often fall

ing into his Pallsie. when after our decease my Children

would be left in an uncertaine condition for Assistance

Behold the goodness of God which taketh care for mine, even

out of strangers. making me see his mercy in his Provi

-dences many fold to me & mine. Praise the Lord o my Soul

and forget not all his benifitts. Amen.

About the 2d of September: 1666 began the great fire

in London , which in 4 days time consumed 13200 houses

89 Churches. &: with out the miraculous Providence

of God it had devoured that whole Citty .
