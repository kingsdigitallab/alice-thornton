In his return home from Newton his own Estate, I was

carried over Hambleton towards Sir will.m Akough s house

where I passed down on foote a very high hill, betwixt

hud hill & whitsonclife , which is a bove a mile steepe down

& indeed so bad, that I could not scarse tread the narrow steps

which was exceeding bad for me in that condition, & sore to in-

-dure the way so strait & none to leade me but my maid ,

which could scarse make shift to get down her self. All our

company being gon down before, each step did very much

streine me being so bigg with child. nor could I have got down

if I had not then bin in my full strength & nimble on foote.

But I bless God I got down safe at last though much tired

& hott & weary, finding my self not well, but troubled with

pains affer my walk: Mr Thornton would not have

brought me that way if he had known it so dangerous. & I

was a strainger in that place. but he was advised by some to

go that way before we came down the hill.

This was the first occasion which brought me a great deal of

Misery & killed my sweet infant in my womb. For I contineu

-ued in all pain by fits upon this jorney. & with in a fortnight

fell into a desperate fever at Hipswell . upon which my old Dr

Mr Mahum was called. but could doe little towards the cure.

because of being with child. I was willing to be ordered by him but

said I found it absolutely necessary to be let blood if they

would save my life. but I was freely willing to resigne my

will to Gods, if he saw fit for me, to spare my life yet to live

with my husband . but still with subservency to my heavenly father

Nor was I wanting to suplicate my God for direction what to
