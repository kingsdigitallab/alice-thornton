An Elegy upon George Wandesford Esquire

on his unfortunate Death. by the Hon.ble Sir Christopher Wyvill Baronet.

On the deplorable loss of our hon.red friend &

Neighbour George Wandesford Esquire March 31 51.

Ere-since the Bishops, Parliament, and King, (A blessed Conspiracy) agreed to bring. The faith of Christians, & Baptismal Seal. Free-Denisons into this Common-Weal. To the late, famous, Stream of Swale adheres, Through the long Current of a Thousand Years. A Sacred-Reputation; there, Whole-Bands Of forward-Converts, by the Reverend-hands Anno.

627.

----

Edwino

Rege. Of Old-Paulinnus did at once Begin To shake-hands with there God, & of their Sin: Those Waves did, then, a font, the Banks afford. An acceptable-Temple to the Lord: Oh What meant the Rash flood by one act, to Throw A Ruin on its own fame, and us too, So brave a vessel, & so Richly fraught. That guilty Channel has to Ship wrack Brought As bankrupts all our Country; Noe-Man here, So unconcerned but must let fall a Tear; Whilst the sadd-murmur of those Waters Call: On every-passenger to mourn his fall: His family; no griefs can tell its fatal Loss;
