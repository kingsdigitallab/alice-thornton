Poor & miserable Life which yet I despisd in comparison

of him; so with much impatience. great fears, & some hope

I waited till night, when word was brought me from Dr wittie

That I should be of good cheare & not cast away my Life

for I should have my dear husband home as well, as ever

I had him in my life.

So that I endeavoured to comfort up my hopes in God, the

Almightie. whose Power was infinite as his mercy &

sweetest Cleamency to us his poor Servants had bin often

showed. And powred out my Prayers & Tears abundan-

-tly that night. for the preservation of the Life & health of my

dear Husband with me. If it were the good will & plea-

-sure of our God. that the dear & sweet union & affectin

intire in our lives together: might not be broke. nor we

seperated by death. from the injoyment of each other. in him

If this might stand with the gracious pleasure of our gratious

God I made these. the requests of my soul to him.

That night was spent in somme little Slumbers but very

unquiett & full of fears trimblings & sad apprehensions

In the morning my Brother Denton came home & very

discreetely prepared me with good advice & counsel to

entertaine the Lords determinate will in all things with

Patience & submittion. If the worst should fall upon me

according to my fears. But with all said that God could raise

My Dearest Joy up again were he never so weak, as I

had Experience of. if he see it fit for us. Although indeed

my dear heart was then very weak. At which words my

faintings renewed with my exceeding sorrows for the fears of

being deprived of this my Sole delight in this world next

under God. The Lord pardon my impatience in this concern.

which had for the 3 last past years, bin weaning him, & my self

from this world, through great & manifold tribulations.

Thus betwixt hopes & fears I remained till the next messen-

-ger came. at 4 a clock on Thursday in the affternoone. at which

time I received the sad newes (for me) of my most Tirrable

Loss that any poor woman could have in being deprived of my
