Be wise & ponder these things, & several other

Providences in these miraculous Deliverances,

to my self, & relations. that the Righteous may know

, & consider the goodness of the Lord, & his loving

kindness, & rejoice in him for ever.

Therefore to the Lord most great, & gracious, be all

Glory, thanksgiving, dominion, & praise, in

heaven & Earth, the Sea & all places of the world

for ever more. Amen. & Amen.

My L.d Straford took Ship for England w:th my Lord Raby his son

on good friday the year. 1640. gaue my father the Sword then.

Observations upon several accidents

happening in Ireland upon the Earle of

Strafford , &c. in the years. 1640, 41. And

on his fatal murder: may: 12 1641.

After my mothers return from the Bathes , &,

Bristoll , where she found much good as to the

cure of the stone.

The Earle of Strafford was sent for by the King in

to England . upon the complaints of some factious

Spirits, weary of a lawful, & Peaceable govern--ment

both in England , Scotland & Ireland , whose

Spirits, & ambitions could not endure a subjection

to their most Pious & good King , nor his Lieutenant

which ruled them in Ireland with a wise & prudential

government, to the preservation of His Majesties Crown.
