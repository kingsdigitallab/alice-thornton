God, or prone of it self to doe any thing unseemly the

Laws of Morallitie or divinitie is very strange Nay I must with all hu-

militie accknowledge the Gift & Grace from God. and with

an unfeined gratitude to his majesty; who hath made

me even from my Youth to abhorre; not only the acts of

these natures. but all inclinations: tendances, or Complian

ces; which might defile the body; or Polute that Precious Soul

Reedemed at so dear a Rate, as by the blood of the Eternal

Son of God. who hath commanded us. be yea holy as I am

Holy: Possessing our vessells in holiness & Righteousness

for ever. And must declare that it has bin my dailie studie

& indeavours to aime at that perfection which is commanded

us. be yea perfect as my heavenly Father is perfect: not

that I dare to imagine tis possible to attaine that full perfecti

on in this life so weak & fraile; yet I follow after, if that

I may possiblie obteine that for which I am aprehended of Christ

Jesus: All though. I daily cast my self down at the foote

stoole (in the true sence of my own unworthinesse;) of Christ

Jesus acknowledging with St Paull though I know nothing by

my self yet am I not thereby Justified. And when I have

don all I can: I am but an unprofittable servant.

Neither would I have presumed to have spoke so much up

pon this subject were I not compelled to set forth Gods Glory

in my preservation & innocency: in the vindication of my

Life from all malicious imputations cast upon me. And

I know that St Paull; when he was dispised spoke more of himself

then otherwise he would have don; And I hope to leave these
