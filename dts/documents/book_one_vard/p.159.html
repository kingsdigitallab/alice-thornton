And Grandmother living then at my Aunts house at

Islelome , being the Eldest Child of my Grandfathers . & in-

tended by him to have inherited his Estate; having so

Intailed it upon her at his first going beyond sea.

in Callis Voyage. After some years he returned into

England, And it pleased God to give them A gallant Son

& heir, which affterwards proved a most Excellent, wise &

Good man, Sir Edward Osborne of Keveton , Barronett.

A faithful prudent man, Zealous for God, the King & the

Church, of great abilitties to serve his King & countery

Being advanced to be Lord President of yorke, &

lived & died in much honour & fame.

To return to my mother , who was bred up in her youth

& infancy with much care & sircumspection by the Eye of

my Grandmother , a discreete & wise woman. giving her

all the advantages of breeding & good education. that the

court, & those times could afford, which was indeed excellent

for gravity, modestie, & piety, & other sutable quali-

ties for her degree, As writing Singing, Danceing. Harpscialls, Lute

&. what was requisit to make her an accomplished Lady

As she did aprove her self in all her time.

At the Age of 21 years , she was married by the consent

of her Mother , being then her self married to Sir

Petter Frechvile , having lived seaven years a widow

since my Grandfathers death; The Portion which my Father

received was very fair in those days being two thou-

-sand Pounds paid the next day of their Marriage.

Nor was she awanting to made a fare greatter improvement

of my Fathers Estate. through her wise & prudentiall

government of his Family, & by her care was a means

to give opportunity of increasing his Patrimony. as

my dear father is pleased to leave upon Record in his

own book for her Eternal hon.our so that it might be

said of her, many Daughters have don well, but thou

exceedest them all.
