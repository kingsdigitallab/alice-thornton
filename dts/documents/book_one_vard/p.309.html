See how it droops, & falls, & almost Dead To see this dreadful Loss of this it's head. Could all our Sighs, & groans, & tears. Prevail, to Call thee back again; our fear -would bee that non could keep thee here The worst of Enemies did be-wail: The loss of so much hon.or in him failed. Since he was dead, the most of worth was gon The Country did cry out; in him, t'was, Oh, Alone: Oh what meant that Rash flood. by one wave to Throw A ruin on its own fame. & us too; So brave A vessel & so Richly Fraught. That guilty River has to Ship-Rack Brought As bankrupts all over Country; No man here So unconcerned. but must let fall A Tear Whilst the sad murmur of those Waters Call. On Every Passenger to mourn his fall What tho three days Submertion did entomb All that was mortal of him in the Womb. Of a Regardless Element we know. Our great Redeemer, from the Parts-Below Did by divine Power, on the third day Rise. To open A nearer way to Paradise. To that blessed Place my Soul desires to Fly Lord then prepare me for it Ere I Dye. Make me to Repent, to watch and Pray. That so I may be Ready for that Day. When thou shalt call this Soul from me. With Joyful hope. I'll come to thee: Amen.
