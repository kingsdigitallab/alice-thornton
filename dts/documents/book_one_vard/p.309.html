See how it droopes, & falls, & almost Dead To see this dreadful Loss of this it's head. Could all our Sighs, & grones, & tears. Prevail, to Call thee back again; our fear -would bee that non could keep thee here The worst of Enimys did be-waile: The loss of so much hon.or in him failed. Since he was dead, the most of worth was gon The Country did cry out; in him, t'was, Oh, Alone: Oh what meant that Rash flood. by one wave to Throw A ruin on its own fame. & us too; So brave A vessell & so Richly Fraught. That guilty River has to Ship-Rack Brought As bank-rupts all over Contry; No man here So unconsernd. but must let fall A Teare Whilst the sad murmur of those Waters Call. On Every Passenger to mourn his fall What tho three days Submertion did intomb All that was mortall of him in the Womb. Of a Regardless Eliment we know. Our great Redeemer, from the Parts-Below Did by divine Power, on the third day Rise. To open A nerer way to Paradise. To that blest Place my Soul desires to Flie Lord then prepare me for it Ere I Dye. Make me to Repent, to watch and Pray. That so I may be Ready for that Day. When thou shalt call this Soul from me. With Joyfull hope. Il'e come to thee: Amen.
