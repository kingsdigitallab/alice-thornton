For my own particular, I was not hasty to change my

free estate, with out much consideration, both as to my

present & future; the first inclining me rather to continue

so still; wherein none could be more satisfied.

The second would contract much more trouble, twisted

inseparably with those comforts God gave in that Estate.

Yet might I be hopeful to serve God in those duties in-

-combant on a, Wife, A mother, a mistress, & governess

in a Family. And if it pleased God so to dispose of me

in marriage making me a more public instrument of

good to those several relations. I thought it rather duty

in me to accept my friends desires for a joint benefit, then

my own single retired content, so that Almighty God

might receive the Glory of my change, & I more capacita-

ted to serve him in this Generation. in what he thus called

me unto.

Therefore it highly concerned me to enter into this great

-est Change of my life with abundance of fear & caution.

not lightly, nor unadvisedly, nor as, I may take my

God to witness that knows the secret of hearts; I did it not

to fulfil the lusts of the flesh. but in chastity, & singleness

of heart, as marrying in the Lord.
