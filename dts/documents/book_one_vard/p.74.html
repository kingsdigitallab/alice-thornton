I was delivered, & hoped God had heard his Prayer

& that you said he might live to the glory of God.

And it did please our gracious father to releive him

out of his missery by Death, about 14 days after

this good child whom he had fitted for himself died

uttering many gracious speeches, out of the scripture,

& abundance of patthaticall prayers, & Petitions to

God: for himself, my mother & us all, with hearty thanks

often to God, who had taken him out of that wicked way

as he calld it, wherein he undoubtedly had bin damnd

bring him to believe aright in God, for his salvation.

with many hopefull & religious expressions, more then

could be expected from such a Child. he freely & will-

-ingly gave up his soul into the hands of his Redeemer

with come Lord Jesus & receive my soul, & so died.

I had great reason to take especiall notice of the great

goodness of God to us, in giving us opportunity to

bring this poor Soul out of the darknesse & ignorance

of his sinful education in which he was. & it was the

good providence of God, so to order it, thus the accident

was. As my father was upon the greene, one day bowling

seeing a poor naked boy in Rags, yet pritty & nimble

was very officious in gathering up his bowles. He took

notice of him with intentions of charity towards the boy.
