That night, came to Hipswell . to consult my mothers

advice about writing to London to Mr Rich Darley

about that business. After his obeisance & craving her

blessing. told her he was now going to Richmond , to my

Uncle will.m where he would write to Mr Darley , desiring to

know what she pleased to command him further in it:

My Mother said that her service & thanks must be returned

him for all his kindness in that bussiness which she would have

don herself by writing. but that she was suddenly surprised

at that instant of his coming up to the Chamber, with much

fears for me, who was, so violently tormented with a

pain in the right side of my neck amongst the sinews &

which caused me to cry out in extremity, nor could she

imagine what was the cause, only she still anointed it with

oil of Roses. My brother seeing me in such pain

Asked how it came. of which I could give no other account

having bin as well before as ever, till I was combeing my

head toward the right hand, & bending my neck as he came

up the stairs, & ever since it had held me grievously.

this was the circumstances of the strange pain, which held me

strongly till about half an hour. which was the very time

of his drowning.

but to proceed to the circumstances of himself. he pitied me

much & would have stayed with me, but that his uncle will.m

stayed for him at Richmond for letters that Post. And after his

walking, 3 or 4 turns about the chamber in his studying

of his business, still my thoughts I saw a great deal of

change, he looked so seriously & soberly. as if there was

some great change near. but what I knew not. only feared

the worst that we should be deprived of him whom I so dealy
