64

Sunday morning. Mackmaughan willing to save

a kinsman of his own name that lived then as a servant

with Sir John Clottworthy an English man, writ his

cousin a letter to Dublin to meet him in great haste

about a business of great concernment.

his cousin immediately took horse & ride into the Country

but finding him gone to Dublin followed, & discovered

where he and Magguire was set drinking in a blind

Alehouse, at which door they had set men to guard it

Now this had bin converted a Protestant about a

year before & married to an English woman . but they

knew not that he was turned. he observed along time

by there ways & impious expressions that they used to

wards the English. so much as he feared some bad

design in hand & therefore was the more diligent

in following them, to discovery.

When they were met they fell to drink much, they

causing him take more that he might be drunk. but

he desired them to tell him what they sent to him

for. Mackmaughan clapped him on the back & told

him that there was the gallantest design which was Plotted

&, to take effect shortly, against the English Dogs,

that could be to cut there throats, & to morrow by 6

a clock in the morning said he my Lord Mackguire

will be master of Dublin Castle . & they would

Batter down the Town over the Heretic Dogs

ears & not spare one of them.

After which speech his cousin the Protestant started,

& cried out, what shall I doe for my wife .
