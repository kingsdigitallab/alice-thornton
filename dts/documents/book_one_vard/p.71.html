Also my Brother John Wandesforde , was preserved

from death in the small Pox. he having taken them of one

of my cosen waill.m wandesford s sonns living then at

Chester . Great was my mothers fear for him, & care

& pains she took about him. and at last he through

mercy was recovered. Although he was very much dis-

figured having bin a very beautifull child. & of a

sweet complextion. In the time of his sickness I was

forbiden to come to him least I should get the small

Pox & indanger my own life. & so observed my mothers

command in that.

But my love for him could not containe it self, from

sending in letters to him, by a way found out of my

foolish invention, tieing them about a little dogs neck

which being taken into his bed, brought the infection of

that dissease upon my self, As also the sight of him

after his recovery. being strooke with fear, seeing him

so sadly used, & all over very read. I immediately

fell very ill, & from that time grew worse.
