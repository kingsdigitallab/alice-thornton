In my book of meditations , there is a discourse upon this deliverance

upon those words in St Matt. 9: v. 21, 22. Luke. the 17: v. 15. 16.

After these weaknesses seized so extremely upon my Person

with such violency & danger to my life, I could not be insensible

of my daily decay & dieing condition: which these frequent

incussions of my health might too suddenly bring to it's

period. Death in it self being desirable to those whose affec

-tions had cause to be weaned from the comforts & vanities

of this Life. wishing to be freed from this worlds troubles. &

to be received into the Arms of Everlasting rest.

yet as a Christian wife, & Mother, was there a duty incu-

-mbent upon me to discharge; with faithfulness & Godliness

towards. my dear husband & Children; according to each Capacity

so that I was obliged to be a comfort to the one. & a support

to the other while I was continued in this world.

Which duties, I made it my study to observe, & though I

could not be in such a state of perfection; doing all I was

commanded from God. yet through his mercy strengthening

me. I may affirm that I made it my endeavour to perform

to my utmost capacity with a good conscience towards all.

And for as much, as the future wellbeing of my Children did

then represent it self to my thoughts; & it had pleased God

in mercy to spare me till that time. living to see my self

blessed with 3 hopeful Children . For whose souls I was the most

concerned in their Education in the true faith of Jesus Christ.

I was the more induced upon this account to accept of a

proposition formerly made; & begun in the year 1665 . By

Mr Comber to my dear husband . that he would except of

him self for my daughter Alice Thornton in marriage.
