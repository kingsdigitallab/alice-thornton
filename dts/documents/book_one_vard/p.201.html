Burne Parke was laid outt in a Rent Charge out of Mr

Laurance Sayers Land of 80l per Annum. This was deput

ed for the Debts: All which did amount to the somme of 3000l

nor did the Debts of Mr Thornton amount to that somme by

600l Neverthelesse by the sequell of all it did appeare; that

This just & honnest action of ours did exasperate such-

with out a cause: who had desires that Laistrop so well as

the Rest of all his Personall Estate should go for security

Colaterall to the rest: And by that means nothing should

be left at certaine for my poor yonger Children.

The case seemed very hard to me: And made me indevor

the more earnestly to prosecute this settlement; which yet I

obteined, with a grand dificulty by reason of these obsta

-cles; but at last prevailed to have it don with out the said

contingency; nor did I intend to Alter the same with out

great cause. which I hoped in God would not be; because there

was so great a somme left for his debts, & was made be-

lieve it could not be cutt of the Intaile with out my consent

but it proved affterward the contrary, to my great grief

& almost my utter Ruin; & my Children's.

There was left only Newton which was my Jointure settled

so on my heir. that it could not be cut off with out my consent

in passing a fine: nor was the Rents ever more clear then

seaven score paid a year. of it.

And now I hartely thanke my gracious God who gave me

opportunity to get this don; for the satisfaction of my

conscience & the relief of my Children; blessed be his name

that did not take me out of this Life before: least by some

means they might have bin disapointed. and I leave

both my husband & Children, all in the wise & gracious

disposittion of the Great God, humbly craveing his blessing

upon them all for Christs sake Amen.

Upon my deliverance, of my son Robert Thornton

my 7th Child: borne at Easte Newton : the first child

that was borne in the new house : Sept. 19th 1662
