And dignities. The Settlement of the Church, & State

upon the right foundations of truth, & peace. Which

these People had no such intentions, as was too

apparent, in the following Rebellions both in England

& Ireland . The Irish being heard to say. That their

Religeion would not prosper as long as Strafford s

head stood on his shouldiers. which saying strongly

proved that this noble Earle was no Patteron of the

Romish Church, although falsely accused so, by

his Seditious Enemies; & his own inncocency was

cleared upon the Scaffold in his speeche made then

But all the discontented parties too well agreed in this one

point, to strike the roote, of the foundations; the King

being aimed at to succeed in this tradegie, As God

knowes to sadly followed;

The Earle of Strafforde haveing far different designes

then those Secret plotters of Rapine, & Ruin.

could not longer be endured, because he stood in the

way to hinder, & prevent by his wise Counsel. So

that till he was removed, they could not prevaille either

in Ireland , or England to compasse their ends.

Nor could there be found, a better expedient for their

purpose then to make a cloake of Religion, that under

such a Populer, specious, pretence, Their horrible

practices might not be found out.
