I hope he now injoys those hapinesse this world is not capab

of. who set himself to seeke God so early & so with an up

right ??? & however that the greatest of Temporal afflictins

[word] SiC ??? i.e., afflictions.



lay heavy on him. yet God sanctified all to him. & gave

him his Perfect use of his reason & understand that half

year before wherein he so excersized himself as was a

great comfort to his friends & relations. & testified that he

gave a return to some of our faithful, prayers for him.

Therefore in all things it now remaines, that I most hartily

return my humble & faithful thanks. Glory & Praise

that heard the groanes of his banished, delivering him by a

quiet & sweet sleep of death from all calamitys of this

wicked world & presed him for him self, who wanted those

mercies which 1000l doeth enjoy. And the Lords name be blessed for

ever that preserved his Precious Soul to his Kingdom through

Jesus Christ. I besech him sanctify this thy hand upon my

brother to me that thereby. I may be drawen nearer to thy

Majesty in love & gratitude. who hast not given me over

in to the will or Power of spirittuall Enemies. or suffered me

to lie in this condition wherein he was vissitted. Lord make

me to admire thy goodness & clemency towards me thy

poor handmaid who had deserved thy heavy hand. but thou

are pleased with testimonys of favour when thy mercy is

provoked that we may further glorify thy name & set forth

thy honnour even to all generations.

Make me O Lord: to esteeme it thy free act of Grace to me

that I have never bin visited in the least with this kind of affliction

for thou alone has preserved me there from. which might have

befalen me as to many of thy dear servants in this world

by there other troubles of this life. O Lord except my humble

thankefull heart & I beseech thee if it be thy will, for

Christs sake never suffer me to fall into this most sad Estate

whereby I may be deprived of the opportunitys of doing thee

service or good to my soul. but give me still the perfect use of

Reason & understanding that I may live all my days to the glory

of thy majesty & the proffitt of my brethren. & the Eternal

good of my own soul for the Lords sake Amen.
