Lamentable condition. being made known to me when

I expected him each hour to have received him home with

health & comfort. Did so surprize my spirits. that I was

brought into a violent passion of grief & sorrow: with fits

of sounding, which I never knew before.

And prevailed so exceedingly, that I immediately went sick

to Bed. being so weak. upon that occasion. that all gave me for

dead. so that it was an impossibility to carry me alive to

see my dear husband . although I could not be pacified

with out it. till, I fell so weak my self I could not speak

Thus was I deprived of seeing, or doing, my faithful duty

to my beloved husband . through the Lords hand upon my

self in such extremity. nor had they hopes of the spai-

ring either of our Lives in all appearance. Only our hopes

was in the miraculous fountaine, of inexhaustable mercies

of the Almighty Lord God. who turneth man to distruc-

tion, & saith turn again from the Grave yee Children of

Men. Nor had I any comfort, or friend. that could

assist me in my Sorrows at that time, nor who to leave my

fower young Children too: in that excigent of both our

sad calamities. but only to the gracious father of Mercies

And beggeing my husbands Relations to have pittie on

my dear Children; then like to be left young Orphans

to the world of troubles.

yet blessed be the Lord who did not give us over to Death

but when we were nigh it behold the goodness of God, was

intreated for us to restore both our lives. And let me ever

return him the Glory of his power, & mercy in the return of

our Prayers. be asscribed to his Majesty. who immediatly

gave us hopes of Recovery. upon calling upon his name.

For that very night about that hour, when we were at prair

for him, my dear husband . wakened as out of sleep

when Dr expected his departure. And at 3 a clock at N.

called for a toste & butter. not having eaten ought of 4

days: & changed so fast in a way of recovery. that it was

admirable to all.
