82

It was advised that my Mother should go from Snape & live at

yorke . for the better Education of my 2 brothers, Christopher

& John . As for my Eldest brother George being then in

France , was happy under the Tuition of one Mr George

Anderson , a scotch man. but a most sober, wise discreet

Person. A great scholar, & excellent qualified man, and

of grand abilities. A Zealous divine for the Church of

England. an indeed a most Excellent good Christian for

his life & conversation.

Under the conduct of this good man , was my deare brother

George , happily placed for his Education, in all good, & commen

-dable qualities, in France , during the heat of the war, in part,

Although he was compelled to return into England for lack

of supplies when his Rents was seized on by the Parliament,

Through which he endured a great deal of hardship. But to return

to my Mother who prepared for Yorke , with her Children

& goods intending to live there. But it pleased God we

was prevented from going further, then a place in the

half way. when we were met with a friend. Mr Danby of

Cave , who gave express notice to my mother . that as she

loved her life. not to go to Yorke . For the Parliament forces

had met with the Kings, & they were all betrayed. & so was

forced to retreat, & that town would be besieged, and so

this counsel came seasonably, & happily, to hinder our greater

troubles & sorrows upon that Town's surprisall. Praised be the

Lord our God which did prevent those evils, & preserved us in our

way. when we were nigh to danger, & knew it not. for this

poor Gentleman Mr Danby was soon after, Killed on the

moore for the King, when the Kings forces was almost all de

-stroyed & cut down by the Scots & the Parliament Army.
