overwhelmed with the stormes of ungodly & wicked doers

whose tongues are like rasiers; sharpe as a two edged sword

Deliver my Soul, O Lord, from lying lips: and from deceit

full tongues which have not God all ways before their Eyes

Lo O Lord they have laine in waite for my Soul. so mall-

iciously were they set against me. And false witness did

rise up: they laid to my charge things that I knew not.

With the flatterers were bussie mockers: which gnashed upon me with

there teeth. They gaped on me with there mouths: & scorne

was in there hearts. Lord how long wilt thou look upon this

O deliver my Soul from the Calamities which they bring on me:

& my darling from the instruments of the Lion and Dragon

Awake & stand up to judge my cause & stand on my side

which am opressed for thy sake. avenge thou my Cause, my

God & my Lord. Make them be asshamed for all those injuri

-ous practices & false accusations against thy innocent

servant: that the Proud doe me no wrong. Judge me, O Lord my

God according to thy mercy & Righteousness. & according to

the cleannesse of my heart in thy eyesight. O let not there

mischeivous imaginations prosper against me least they be

to proud. let them not triumph against my soul. for thou only

searchest & triest the thoughts. neither let them triumph over

me for I am thy servant & humble creature redeemed by thy

Precious blood. I beeseech thee O Lord let not their lieing

slanders prevaille to make me miserable, either to blemish

my Person family or Posterity. For thou knowest that against

these People have I don no wrong, that is risen up against me

but allwaies ready to releive there necessitys & wants. And lo

now they take my contrary part. let not there imaginations

prosper because I put my trust in thee. Plead thou my cause

O Lord with them that strive with me: & fight thou against them

that fight against me & persecute me: say unto my soul

I am thy salvation, Yea O Lord our God, who art the sheild of

the oppressed & wronged. the buckler of all that trust in thee. de

liver us from all the assaults & intendments of our secret malicius

Enimes against us, who with out cause make pitts for our souls

let the Angell of the Lord scatter all mischeivous immaginations

least they triumph over us & say, we have devoured them.
