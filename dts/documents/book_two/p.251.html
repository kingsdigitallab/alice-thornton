bring him out of those straits by Passing a fine of
Burne Parke , which he had bond monney on of Mr

Pay nettleton , and if I would not Consent to Joyne
in a fine before Barron Thorpe that day, he saw noe
way but to goe to Jale for my Father s Debt.

This speech did much Terrify me to be soe sudani-
ly Surprised and, of soe sad a nature, beeing great with
Childe I fell into a great Sorrow for it.

Both for the misfortune of my Husband , and
