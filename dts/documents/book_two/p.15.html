15

In this schoole of affliction. have I bin exerzised
by the good providence of God. Ever since I was
borne. that soe I might by(?) Sic for 'be'. kept from the wayes of the
wicked & the practice of those that knows not God.
But such has bin his caire & providences over me
That I have cause to blesse, & praise the name of
the Lord for every twigg of his Rod That soe he might
make me conformable to his Son & keepe from me
the vanitys of this world to serve him in newnes
of life & conversation.

Yett so has bin his Goodness to me: that I have bin noe sooner
freed from one trouble. but annother hath arrised
out of it's Ashes. his wise dispensation, has bin
mixed his favours with frownes. his mercys, with
Trialls, & Afflictions, to me, his unworthy servant
That there has bin noe time allotted for Sin to rule
in this heart with his deadly poyson As in such
vessells which are settled on there Lees.

This I speake, not to boast. nor glory of any thing in
this world. save in the Crosse of my Christ which has
loved me & given himselfe for me. That I might
be kept from the Evill of this world. to magnifie his
holy name.
