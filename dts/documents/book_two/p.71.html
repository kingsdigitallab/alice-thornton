The poore Clarke died, and Mr Kerny still

kept the Chest under safe Custody, and non

came to inquire for there writings, & there

was such a disturbance in Ireland, & that

City of Dublin. That till there was somme

Peace & respit from troubles he did not see

fitt to looke into the Chest:

But now as it was soe ordered by our

good God in his Providence for our reliefe.

was the time, that Mr Kerney did first

open this Chest, finding many Deeds, and

wills, & Evidences, put up very safely.

He takeing them all out of the Chest till

he came at the very botom, of it, And finds

A large Stately writings in 5 sheets of

Parchment and looking at the Bottome

The name of my Lord Deputy Wandesford
