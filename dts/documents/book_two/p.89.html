now since such danger, & misery, that accrewed

to me, by the act of the first Adam: O Lord I humbly

desire that I may take hold of the covenant of grace

which the second Adam hath prepared for us.

And that I may ever more magnify thy great &

unspeakable mercies, in sending thy only Sonn to

become man, for us, whoe being with out sin, suffered

for sinn, That, we whoe were nothing but Only sin

might be made ritch by the imputation of his

Allsuficient righteousness.

But deare father of mercyes such is my

infirmities, & weakness, that I have been so farre

from acknowledging thy infinit goodness towards us

in sending, thy only son to redeeme us, when we

were utterly lost, so farre from beeing thankfull to

him for his unspeakable love to me, that was contented to

suffer, even the scornfull, death of the Crosse for my

sake. so farre from the participation of his vertues

or the immitation of his vertues, & holiness; & examples

That those Lawes, which either by the instinct of

nature, or by divine precept thou hast carractred in

our hearts. I have in as much as in me laid rased out

by ading actuall to orriginall Sinne:

O Lord I humbly cast my selfe downe before thy

majesty, not only for my owne sins &, transgressions.
