Aprill the 12th 1660 But I was in much & great

Extreamitys affter his birth uppon the midwifes

fetching the Affterbirth and for halfe an hour

I was in such extreamity while she did fetch it

That my deare Aunt cryed out Oh what are

you doeing with her for the Child is come. which she

tooke ill at my Aunt because she thought she used me soe hard

hardly affter the birth of the Childe.

Thus was it the good pleasure of the great &

Powerfull Lord God of mercy to shew his favr

to me his handmaide in brnging me out of all those

Tortures, of Childe-beaing & what did belong unto it

and give me annother sweete & gooly Sic for 'goodly'. Son.

to be borne alive & hopefull enough to have

lived with us to have built up his deare fathrs

Family. And I will humbly give All glory &

thankes & praise for this great & inexpresable

mercys to me in giving strength to indure these

trialls & to bing forth Children to his Kingdom.

O what shall I say, o my God who called on thee in

my distresse, & thou didst delvr me of this time allso

& gave me the comfort and assistance of my Aunt &

othr freinds Lord give me a thankfull heart & tongue

to serve thee all my dais & not forgett thy mercy forer I.e. 'foreuer'.
