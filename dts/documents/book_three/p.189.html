To which answer of Mr Thornton . Mr Comber being

made acquainted affter his motion. returnd him many

Thankes for his good oppinion of him and that he would

endeavour to deserve her. And if it Pleased him to give

his consent for him he would stay for her if it were.

seaven years soe he might obteyne thatt happiness.

 21.

Affter which answer of Mr Comber s to my deare

husband s kindness. of his good oppinnion for him. Mr

Thornton made answer That he would give his con-

sent if he would thinke fitt to stay Till she was at

Age sutable to enter in to a married Estate.

23.

Mr Comber s first letter to yorke to my daughter

Alice when she went to Learne Quallityes with Kate her sistr

and my made hannah to waite on them.

 1. lettr

May

25 67.

 In which he really professeth his most indeared Affection

for her & her vertues which obleiges his admiring her modesty

& other graces & beggs she will declaire her thoughts to him.

 2.

Mr Comber s 2d letter June 19 1667 To his hon.red

Lady Mrs (Alice Comber) Thornton To yorke , his most

Earnest desire to see her, and of his feare the newes of his

sickness should trouble her his growing more strong and

hearty. hopes to waite on her shortly. his writing to Mrs sherwood

of some differences towards her & hanna

3.

Mr Comber s 3d letter July 19 1667 to my daughtr

Alice to yorke affter his recrut & giving her a vissitt at yorke

Her very great sattisfaction in her late vissitt wherein

she had obleiged him to be Etternally hers & To have long

since cast of all motions what ever or any other. his desire to

have her return home to Newton . his owning himselfe unworthy

of her Affection, tho he is unallterable, &c
