(For noe sooner was the Trajedy of my honour Acted
The Relation of which, is in my first booke; related
with severall happy sircumstances in order to my
vindication, uppon my first knowledge by what
meanes I came into that sad misfortune.) But
devine Providence soe ordered, allso, That, all the
Parties, which was accused by Mrs Danby, & her
accursed instrument Barbara Todd. which was
in my house, was brought before my brother
Denton, & Examined. of what they were charged with all;
And all with a full consent did utterly deny any
thing of that nature. And uppon there oathes did
vindicate there wronged mistress from those
abuces. with great indignation against the first
authours of that wickedness.

And then Barbra did fly to accuse one maide
which was gon away to be the raiser of those slandrs
which woman was written to by Dafeny to know the
truth of such reports.

But the said woman did utterly deny. That she
did ever know or see any evill by me in all her
life & did admire att there impudence which should
Raise any such scandall against her Mrs & her-
-Selfe.
