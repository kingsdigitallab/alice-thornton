149 1668.

My dear deare Husband Will.m William Thornton

Esquire went to Malton to sister Portington

on friday fridy the 11th of September 1668 . &

was much as he used to be of late pretty pritty

well of his infirmity. went to malton Hill

on the next day . & to church on Sunday .

upon Monday mundy he was not well & had Pills

given him by bro.r brother Portington . sent for Mr

Sinkler . & told tould him that he knew that formerly

he had been bin in much trouble of mind minde &

sadness sadnesse for his sins & had walked uncom

-fortably for the want of the sense sence of

Gods favour. & in great fears feares & horrors

but now the Lord was pleased to make himself him

-selfe known knowne to him to be a reconciled reconsiled

father in Christ. & that he was at Peace

with him & did feel feele a great deal deale of

Joy & comfort inwardly in his soul soule

& mind minde . Blessing the Lord for these

his infinite infinitt mercies mercys & hoped that he should

bless blesse his name forever for ever more that he was

troubled. for now he was reconciled reconsiled to

him again againe . & a great many of such li

like expressions as these was to the great

satisfaction sattisfaction of Mr

Sinkler
