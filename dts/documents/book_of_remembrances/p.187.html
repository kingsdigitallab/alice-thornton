She did see in the north window in her
Chambr the sky broake out & opned with a
dreadfull noyse. & like gunnes & swordss to
warre. when followed An infinit numbr
of People like common People & meanre meaner sort
with Raw bond faces like Scotts. & Irish, &
English all in a great confusion & hurry
as if runnig with Armes & a posture of
fiting & other instruments of warre. These
came before with loud clamors & shouts. &
noyse. Then a little distance after came
my Lord of Strafford the Lord Leiftenant
of Ireland. in mourning habit. & most
sad & Pensive walked at a space by him
selfe: first. & then apeared as with out a
head: Affter him the Bishop of Canterbury soe allso.
After my Lord at a good pretty distance
Apeared the perfect Person of the good
King Charles the first with the Crowne on his
head, but beeing Pursued he looked backe
in a dread fright goeing fast on before. And
haveing his sword Draune in one hand. &
The other Arme he held over the young
Prince Charles in defending & preserving
him him from the multitude and this in a
great Conserne. & seemd to fly. & having
his Robes & Crowne on his head.
