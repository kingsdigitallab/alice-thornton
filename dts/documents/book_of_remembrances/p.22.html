20 1639.

Having come over into England

with my d. dear mother to the bath for the stone,

in our return returne back to Ireland , August

22th 39 , . I, with the rest of the ship Ship and &

the passengers, was delivered delivired from that

great and & terrible terable Sorme storm in Neston nesston

water , where there was five 5 ships cast

away before we took tooke ship. But but , the

same great God that maketh the stomes storms

to cease seace brought us safely safly out of that

storm storme . and & carried caried us well to the land Land ,

though in great fright and & much

distress distresse . I praise the Lord . who brought

my soul soule out of the deep deepe waters and &

fathomless fathomlesse gulf gulfe of waters. Praise praise

his holy name for ever and & for ever Amen

I had a second preservation from drowning

out of the ship at that time, when a

cable had like to have pulled puld me into

the sea but for a ship man that saved

me as I was half halfe over. Bless blesse the Lord,

O o my soul soule , and & forget not the deliverance deliveranc

Amen.
