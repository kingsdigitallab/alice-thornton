My Life a death, but in his Love; My Death a life for him to Prove; My care to keepkeepe a Conscience cleancleane;[1] My Will from wicked thoughts to weanweane; My PainPaine, and pleasure, travailtravell, ease, My God thus in all things to please; Nor Earth, nor Heaven should me move, But Still my Lord should be my love. If I am sicksicke, he were my health, If I am poorpoore, he were my wealth. If I am weakweake, he is my strength, If dead, he is my life at length; If sorne'dscorned, he onlyonely is my grace; If banished, he my Resting-Place. If wronged, he only is my Right. If Sad, he were my Soul'sSoules delight; In sumsumme, and all, alonelyAll-onely hehee, Should be all, abovea bove All to memee.
