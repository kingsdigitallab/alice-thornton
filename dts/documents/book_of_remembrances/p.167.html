A Prayer.

O Lord God of our Salvation, who for

our Sakes were wounded & didst didest

die to redeem redeeme our Souls Soules from hell.

& wast pleased to lie in the Grave, that

our sins sinnes might be buried by thee, by

& act of oblivion. but thou alone of all

that ever died were free amongst the dead

to show shew thy Sovereignty Soveranity over all, & of

thine own owne Power didst arise again againe

with victory & triumph; O Powerful Powerfull

omnipotent Lord God, regard I humbly

beseech besseech thee, the pourings out of a weak

frail fraile despised handmaid hanmaid of thine, have

mercy upon thy poor poore Servant, whom thou

hast made, & preserved by thy power

& might hitherto, lo loe thine indignation

Lieth hard upon me, & thou hast vexed

me with all thy storms stormes ; thy hand presseth

me sore ; My soul soule is full of trouble by

reason of my Sins Sinnes , & my life draweth

nigh
