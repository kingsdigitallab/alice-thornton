91 1665

But peradventure I might be too

much lifted up by this mercy. & there

-fore it seemed good to the most wise

providence of our Creator. to lay a

very sad affliction upon Mr Thornton

& my self selfe in a most dangerous sickness sickenese

seizing seizeing upon him as he was at Yorke

about the 16th of novemb.r November 1665

when he could not come home because

of the violency of cold, & the prevailing prevaling

distemper growing more upon him in his

Journey Jorney . so that he was forced to be at Stersby

my sister Cholmleys house.

For three days daies that grievous greevious distemper

of the Palsy Palsey , . convulsions convolsions , . and fever feavour

was so high upon him that notwithstanding

all remedies remidies that could be used by Dr wittie

that he was not capable to assist himself himselfe

or indeed some times of what others did for

him. it seeming to all his friends freinds there present

that he hee was more like to die. than then hopes of

life.
