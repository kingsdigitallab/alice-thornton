At which he gave a sight sigh , and said for my sake sak

he would not venture again againe . but have a care

of him self & gave me his hand he would not go goe

over our it again a gaine when it was high. which pleased me

to hear heare this Resolution: nor did he ever evr till that

day of his death gone gon that way after affter : And then

was compelled compelld to it by Reason of shortness of time.

It was on Easter Monday Munday . when he came from Mr

Darcys (Henry) : he having the Easter day by Gods

mercy been bin prepared prepard to Recav Receive the Holy Sacrament

& to my great Joy did take it with my dear deare mother mothr

& my self selfe & my brother brothr Christophr , one thing

must not be forgotten. That there had been bin a great

falling faling out betwixt G. W: & C: W my brothers brothrs about

the menservants that made debate amongst them

so soe that they would not have received recavd that holy feast.

But it pleased God to give me that blessing. That I did

use all my utmost uttmost endeavours endeavors to Reconcile Reconsile them

by all Christian Persuasion Perswasion . which I humbly Praise God

for I did at that time And they were in perfect Charity

each to others othrs & did both receive receave very Reverently

on Easter day in Hipswell Chapell with my D. mother mothr

& my self selfe . But in the afternoon Affter noone my brother brothr

G: W: did go goe to see Mr Henry Darcy . and find ing

him not at home, entertained entertand him self selfe with old motther>

Darcy . & he took tooke a Bible and read a chapter to her

& did make such an admirable exposition on it
