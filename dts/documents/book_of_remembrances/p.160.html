Rich hated; wise suspected; scornd . if poore Great feared; faire tempted; high still envied more would the world then adopt me for her heire. Would beauties queene intitle me the faire. Fames speake me ho.nrs minnion & could I With Indian-Angells, & a speaking Eye. Command bare heads; bow'd knees strike Justis dumb As well as blind & lame, & give a Tongue To stones by Epitaphs; be calld great master In the last lines of every Poetaster: Could I be more then any man that lives Great, wise, rich faire . all in Superlatives Yet I these favours would more free resigne Then ever Fortune would have had them mine. I count one minute of my holy leasure Beyond the mirth of all this Earthy pleasure wellcome pure thoughts. come the Sober groves These are my guests: this is the court I loves. The winged people of the skies, shall sing. me Anthems by my Sellers gentle spring.
