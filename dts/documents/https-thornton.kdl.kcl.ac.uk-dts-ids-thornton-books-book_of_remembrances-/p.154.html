154 Since natuers workes be good, and, Death doth serve. As natures worke, why shold we feare to die. Since feare is vaine, but when it may preserve, Why should we feare that which we cannot flie. Feare is more paine than is the paine it feares. Disarming humaine mindes of native might. While each conseipt an ugly figure beares. Which were not evill, well viewd in reasons light Our onely eyes, which dimmd with passions be. And scarce discerne the dawne of comming day.
