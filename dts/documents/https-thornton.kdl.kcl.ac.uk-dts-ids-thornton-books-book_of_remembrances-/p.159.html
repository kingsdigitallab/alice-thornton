159 A prayer booke shall be my looking glasse Wherein I will adorne sweete vertues face Heare dwells noe heartlesse loves noe pale fact feares Noe short Joyes purchast with Eternall tears. Heere will I sit & sigh my weakes youth folly. And learne to affect an holy mallancholly. And if contentment be a stranger, then He neare looke for it but in Heaven agaen.

The Just shall live by faith.

Nisi Christus Nemo. Tout pour le’ Eg’lize. Christ and the Church, in love so well ag’reed That hee for her, & she for him has Blee’d. Thus imitate thy Saviour, in his fervent love And then my Soule her Joyes will lasting prove Oh groundles deeps, o love without degree The offended dies, to Sett the offender free.
