160 My Soules Wish. Oh: Had I of his love but Part, That chosen was by Gods owne heart, That Princely Prophett David hee, Whom in the word of truth I see. The King of Heaven so dearely love’d. As mercy beyond measure prov’ed; Then should I neither Gyant feare, Nor Lyon that my Soule would teare; Nor as the Philistims, nor such friends: As never, were true Christians friends: Noe passions should my Spiritt vexe. Nor sorrow so my minde perplex. But I should still all glory give. Unto my God by whom I live. And to the Glory of his Name, Throughout the world divulge the Same: My walke should be but in his wayse, My talke but onely in his Praise.;
