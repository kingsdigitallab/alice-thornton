144 1668.

And daly pant & long to be
Clothed upon that death might
putt on Imortality. spending a
few daies heere amongst thy children
in Pietie & holinesse. to bring them
up in the feare & nuture of the Lord
that I may delivr up my Charge to
the Great God of Heaven with Joy
& not with greife. & in the end of my
daies receave the end of my hope even
the Salvation of my Soule. where there is
noe sighing. noe weeping. for all tears
shall be wiped from our Eyes. then sh
shall be love with out hatred. Joy witho
sorrow. delight. without anger. hopes that
is turned to injoyment with out feares.
to losse, or to to be lost. noe caires. nor Troubble
noe hatred. nor envy shall enter there
for in Heaven they are as the Angells
nay higher. & drawne nearer in beeing
drawen & united to the nature of God
in the second person of the Trinity. the Eternall
